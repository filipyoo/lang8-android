
Using ～ほどじゃない/ほどではない

～ほどじゃない, which is a casual version of ～ほどではない, means "not so ～ as ～." ほど follows a noun to compare.

カナダの寒さはロシアほどじゃない。
- Canada isn't so cold as Russia.

この料理はおいしいけど寿司ほどじゃない。
- This dish is good, but isn't so delicious as sushi.

プロのサッカー選手はみんな足が速いけど、ボルトほどじゃない。
- Every professional soccer player can run fast, but can't run as fast as Bolt does.


It also can follow a verb to mean "not deserved to."

このレストランの料理はおいしいけど、三ツ星を取るほどじゃない。
- The dishes in this restaurant are good, but I don't think they deserves to get three stars.

彼の作品は悪くはないけど、「芸術」と呼ぶほどじゃない。
- His work isn't bad, but it doesn't deserved to be called "art."

彼は確かにミスをしたけど、クビにするほどじゃない。
- It's true that he made a mistake, but it's too much to fire him.

その研究は面白いけど、大金を投資するほどじゃない。
- The research is interesting, but I don't think it's worth to invest a lot of money.
