
Using だって

When you describe a conclusion and the reason in Japanese, you usually say the reason first:

疲れているから、買い物に行きたくない。
I don't want to go shopping because I'm tired.

If you want to say a conclusion first, you can use だって between the conclusion and the result.

買い物に行きたくない。だって、疲れているから。

In this case, you can't describe them in one sentence.

だって sounds slightly childish, you should use なぜなら or なぜかというと instead in a formal situation.

オバマは次の大統領選挙に出れない。なぜなら、すでに２期務めているから。
- Obama can't run for president again because he has already served two terms.


だって can be used to describe a hearsay. ん is placed before だって in this case.

彼と彼女は来月結婚するんだって。知ってた？
- They say that he and she are going to get married next month. Did you know?

彼はまた逮捕されたんだって。
- I heard that he was arrested again.


だって is also used to mean "even."

子供だってこの問題は解ける。
- Even children can solve this problem.

大人だってこの問題は解けない。
- Even adults can't solve this problem.

足を骨折したから、歩くことだってできない。
- I broke my leg, so I can't even walk.

彼はすごい金持ちで飛行機だって買える。
- He's so rich that he can even buy a plane.
