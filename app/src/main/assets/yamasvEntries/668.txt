
The Difference Between 〜が〜てある and 〜を〜てある

てある is used when you did something and the situation as the result of the action has not changed. 朝食を作った means that you made breakfast, and the listener can't know whether  you ate it or not. On the other hand, 朝食を作ってある means that you made breakfast and you haven't eaten it yet. てあった means that the situation has changed.

〜が〜てある means that someone else did the action. 〜を〜てある means that the speaker did that.


冷蔵庫にワイン「が」買ってある。
- There is wine someone bought in the refrigerator.

冷蔵庫にワイン「を」買ってある。
- I bought wine and keep it in the refrigerator.


窓「が」開けてある。暑いから誰かが開けたのだろう。
- The windows are open. Someone must have opened them because it's hot.

暑いから窓「を」開けてある。
- I opened the windows because it's hot.


変な車が家の前に駐まっている。誰も乗っていないのにエンジン「が」かけてある。
- There is a strange car in front of my house. Even though no one is in the car, the engine is started.

今朝はとても寒いから、15分前から車のエンジン「を」かけてある。
- I started the engine of my car 15 minutes ago because it's very cold this morning.


いい絵「が」ホテルの部屋に掛けてある。
- There is a beautiful picture on the wall in my hotel room.

僕の部屋には家族の写真「を」たくさんかけてある。
- I hang a lot of pictures of my family on the wall in my room.
(僕の部屋には家族の写真「が」たくさんかけてある is technically correct, but it means that someone else hung them.)


Some people may sometimes say 〜が〜てある even though they themselves did it in order to describe the situation objectively.  However, 〜を〜てある always means that the speaker did it.
