
は and が with a Negative Form

は is usually used with a negative form.

雨は降っていません。
- It's not raining.

社長はここにはいません。
- The president isn't here.


When が is used with a negative form, it implies that the noun before が is something important or you are supposed to have.

雪がないから、スキーはできません。
- You can't ski because there's no snow.

社長が来てないからミーティングを始められない。
- We can't start the meeting because the president hasn't come yet.


私の家には、サウナはありません。
私の家には、サウナがありません。
- We don't have a sauna in our house.
The latter sounds unnatural because the typical house doesn't have a sauna. The latter sounds as if most houses have a sauna.

私の家には、トイレはありません。
私の家には、トイレがありません。
- We don't have a bathroom in our house.
The former sounds unnatural because the typical house has a bathroom. 

ポルシェを買う金はない。
ポルシェを買う金がない。
- I don't have money to buy a Porsche.
The latter sounds unnatural. It sounds as if a Porsche is cheep or a lot of people have it.

バスに乗る金はないから歩いて帰った。
バスに乗る金がないから歩いて帰った。
- I walked home because I didn't have a bus fare.
The former sounds unnatural. It sounds as if the fare is expensive.
