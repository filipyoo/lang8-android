
Using あっさり

あっさり can be used to describe food taste. あっさりした味 means light taste.

ラーメンが好きだけど、ラーメンはこってりしている。だから時々そばみたいなあっさりした食べ物が食べたくなる。
- I like ramen, but it's heavy, so I sometimes want to eat light taste food like soba.


It's also used to describe your character. あっさりした人 means a good sport, not square, not stubborn.


あっさり can be also used as an adverb to mean "easily." It's often used when you did something that is supposed to be difficult.

彼は再び、あっさり金メダルをとった。
- He won the gold medal again easily.

彼は横領をあっさり認めた。
- He easily admitted the embezzlement.

どの教授も解けなかった数学の問題をその学生はあっさり解いた。
- The student solved the math problem easily, which any professor couldn't.


You can use あっさりと instead. There is no major difference between them.
