
How to Agree in Japanese

How to agree in Japanese is very simple. You can always say "私も." It doesn't depend whether the first speaker said positive or negative form.

I like cats. / Me too.
私は猫が好きです。 / 私も。

I don't like dogs / Me neither.
私は犬が嫌いです。 /  私も。

I can speak Japanese. / So can I.
日本語が話せます。/ 私も。

I can't speak English well. / Neither can I.
英語がうまく話せません。/ 私も。

Of course, you can say "私も猫が好きです," "私も犬が嫌いです" or so on. However, they sound lengthy. Most Japanese people would say just "私も" in casual conversation.


If you disagree the first speaker's opinion, you should say "私は〜."

私は猫が好きです。- I like cats.
私は嫌いです。- I don't.

This は in "私は嫌いです" is used not as a topic marker but to show a contrast.
あなた「は」猫が好きですが、私「は」嫌いです。 

私は猫が好きです。- I like cats.
私は、猫は好きですが犬は嫌いです。- I like cats too, but I don't like dogs.
I can't say that "猫「が」好きですが犬「が」嫌いです" is incorrect, but "猫「は」好きですが犬「は」嫌いです" sounds more natural.
