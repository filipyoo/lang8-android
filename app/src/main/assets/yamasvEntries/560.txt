
Using 〜ばかりか and 〜どころか

〜ばかりか is used to mean "not only 〜 but 〜." It's mainly used in positive sentences.

彼は日本語ばかりか、フランス語も話せる。
- He can speak not only Japanese but also French.

彼女は美しいばかりか、賢い。
- She is not merely beautiful but smart.

彼は私を家に泊めてくれたばかりか、お金までくれた。
- Not only did he put me up in his house, he also gave me some money.


〜どころか is mainly used in negative sentences.
 
私はバイクどころか、自転車も乗れない。
- I can't ride a bicycle, let alone ride a motorcycle.
(バイク means a motorcycle not a bicycle in Japanese.)

新しいコーチは、チームを良くするどころかむしろ悪くした。
- The new coach made the team worsen rather than improve.

徹夜で勉強しても、効果的どころかマイナスの結果になりうる。
- Studing through the night can be a negative result rather than a beneficial one.

この教科書は、役に立たないどころか有害だ。
- This textbook is worse than useless.
