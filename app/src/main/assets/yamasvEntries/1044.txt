
Using ～どころ

When どころ(所) follows a noun or the masu-stem of verbs, it means "the most important moment/part/point."

マラソンは35Km付近が勝負どころだ。
- In a marathon, the turning point to win is about 35 Km mark.

今が頑張りどころだ。
- It's crunch time.

今が我慢のしどころだ。
- Now is the time to grin and bear it.

この映画の見どころのひとつはカーアクションだ。
- One of the movie's highlights is a car action.

この歌の聞きどころは彼女のソロだ。
- The most beautiful part of this song is her solo.

「よろしく」の使いどころがわからない。
- I don't know when should I say "よろしく".

このジョークの笑いどころが理解出来ない。
- I don't understand this joke. / I don't understand the point to laugh at of this joke.

交渉の際は落としどころを探すのが大切だ。相手の利益も考えないとうまくいかない。
- It's important to find common ground when you negotiate. You won't succeed if you don't consider the opponent's benefit.
