
Using あれこれ and あちこち

あれこれ means "this and that." The order is different from that in English. (あれ means "that" and これ means "this" in Japanese.)

その刑事は私にあれこれ質問していった。私が犯人だと思っているのかも。
- The detective asked me this and that. They might think that I was the criminal.

私は若いころあれこれ色んな仕事をしました。
- I worked this and that when I was young.


あちこち means "here and there."

今は、あちこちで桜が見られます。
- You can see cherry blossoms here and there now.

あちこちそれを探したけど、見つからなかった。
- I looked for it here and there, but I wasn't able to find it.

日本では、あちこちに自動販売機があります。
- In Japan, there are vending machines everywhere.

あちこち is a casual expression, あちらこちら sounds slightly polite.
