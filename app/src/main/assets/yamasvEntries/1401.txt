
Using 事実上

"事実上(じじつじょう)の" means "virtual/de facto." It's used when something is technically different from the fact, but everybody considers it in fact.

彼は副社長だが、全ての重要な決定は彼が行っている。彼は事実上の社長だ。
- He's a vice-president of that company, but he makes every important decisions; he's the virtual president.

世界ランク1位のジョコビッチと2位のマレーが準決勝で対戦する。事実上の決勝と言っていい。
- Djokovic, who is the world top ranker, and Murray, who is the second, will face in the semifinals; you can say that it's the de facto final.


You can use 事実上 as an adjective:

その選手は来シーズンはプレーしないと言った。彼はもう42歳だから、事実上、引退すると言っているのに等しい。
- The player announced that he wouldn't play the next season. It's equal to say that he'll retire because he's already 42 years old.
