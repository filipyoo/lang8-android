
Usage of くれる and もらう

くれる and もらう are used when someone gives you something. The difference between them is who the subject is.

彼が、チョコをくれた - He gave me a chocolate.
彼から、チョコをもらった - I was given a chocolate from him.

They are also used when someone does something for you.

彼が、日本語を教えてくれた - He taught me Japanese language.
彼から、日本語を教えてもらった - I was taught Japanese language from him.


Furthermore, くれる is used when someone's action or something makes you happy.

本田が次の試合でACミランのためにゴールを決めてくれると思う。
- I believe that Honda will score a goal for A.C. Milan in the next match.
You can say this Japanese sentence even if Honda and you don't know each other. This Japanese sentence implies that you are a fan of A.C. Milan because くれる is used. If you tell your guesses from an unbiased stance, you should say "ゴールを決めると思う." 

雨が止んでくれたので、公園でサッカーを楽しめた。
- We enjoyed soccer in that park because the rain had stopped.

スマートフォンが時間を教えてくれるから、腕時計はしない。
- I don't wear a wristwatch because my smart phone tell me what time it is.

赤ちゃんがぐっすり寝てくれるから、今夜は読書を楽しめる。
- I can enjoy reading tonight because our baby sleeps well.


On the other hand, you can't use もらう when the action doesn't aim to make you happy. The following sentences is technically correct but sounds a little weird.
? 雨に止んでもらったので、公園でサッカーを楽しめた。
? スマートフォンに時間を教えてもらえるから、腕時計はしない。
? 赤ちゃんにぐっすり寝てもらえるから、今夜は読書を楽しめる。
The rain and smart phone don't have concious, and the baby doesn't plan to help you.

If a police officer happend to overlook your red light running, you can say "信号無視を見落としてくれた," but you can't say "信号無視を見落としてもらった." The latter sentence sounds as if the officer intentionally ignore your violation.
