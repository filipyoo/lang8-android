
Using 結局～

結局～ means "to end up ～."

今日彼の家に遊びに行く約束をしたけど、結局行かなかった。
- I promised him to visit his place today, but ended up not going there.

結局どの車を買ったの？
- Which car did you end up buying?

2つのことを同時に得ようとすると、結局1つも得られない。
- If you try to get two things at the same time, you'll end up getting nothing.

昨日まで決心がつかなかったけど、結局仕事を辞めた。
- Although I hadn't made up my mind until yesterday, I ended up quitting my job.


When you describe negative result, you can say "はめになった". If you say "仕事を辞めるはめになった," it implies that you didn't want to quit the job. 

上司のミスのせいで、(結局)残業するはめになった。
- Because of my boss's mistake, I ended up working overtime.
In this case, you can omit 結局 because はめになった implies it.
