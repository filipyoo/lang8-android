
Using けち

けち means to be stingy. You can write it in both hiragana and katakana.

僕の上司はけちだ。コーヒー一杯おごってくれたことがない。
- My boss is stingy. He never has even bought me a cup of coffee.


けちる is an informal verb used when you do something to save money. It has a negative nuance.

タクシー代をケチって歩いて会社に行ったせいで打ち合わせに遅刻した。
- I was late for the meeting because I walked to the office to save the taxi fare.

私が毎日弁当を持ってくるのは、昼ごはん代をけちるためじゃなくて、健康のためです。
- I bring bento every day not because to save the money, but because it's good for my health.


けちをつける means to throw cold water.

僕の上司はいつも僕の計画にけちをつける。
- My boss always throws cold water on my plan.
