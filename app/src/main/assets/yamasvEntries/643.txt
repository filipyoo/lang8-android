
Using ～め

め can follow the stem part of some i-adjectives to mean "a little more ～ than average one or than usual."
For example, 太め means a little fatter than average size.

あそこの少し太めの男性が私の父です。
- A little fat man over there is my father.

今朝は早めに家を出た。
- I left home a little earlier than usual this morning.

この帽子は小さすぎる。大きめのはないですか？
- This hat is too small for me. Do you have a bigger one?

小さめのバッグがほしい。
- I'd like a little bit smaller purse.

髪が短めの男性が好きです。
- I like men with short hair.

このレストランは値段が他の店より少し高めです。
- This restaurant is a little more expensive than others.

彼女にいつもより多めにチップをあげた。
- I tipped her a little more than usual.
