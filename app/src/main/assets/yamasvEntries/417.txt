
Usage of 〜だらけ

〜だらけ means "to be covered with 〜." It follows nouns.

コインをなめちゃダメ！ばい菌だらけだよ。
Don't lick coins, they are covered with germs.

サッカーの試合が終わった後の弟のシャツは泥だらけだった。
My brother's shirt was covered with mud after the soccer game was finished.

彼は血だらけでその道路に倒れていた。
He was covered with blood and laying down the load.

You can use まみれ instead of だらけ in this case.
コインをなめちゃダメ！ばい菌まみれだよ。
サッカーの試合が終わった後の弟のシャツは泥まみれだった。
彼は血まみれでその道路に倒れていた。


It's also used to mean "there are a lot of 〜." 

彼の部屋はアニメのDVDだらけだ。
There are a lot of anime DVDs in his room.

先月パリに行ってきたけど日本人だらけだった。日本にいるみたいだった。
I visited Paris last month, but there were a lot of Japanese people there. I felt as if I was in Japan.

この教科書は間違いだらけだ。買うんじゃなかった。
This textbook is full of mistakes. I shouldn't have bought it.

These may also have the nuance of "to be covered with 〜" a little, but you can't use まみれ instead of だらけ in this case. まみれ can be used only when the surface is covered with something.
