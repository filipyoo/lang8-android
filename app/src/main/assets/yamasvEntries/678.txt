
Using 以上

〜以上 means "more than 〜."

あなたの日本語は思ってた以上に上手です。
- Your Japanese is better than I thought.

あなたの日本語は普通の日本人以上にうまい。
- Your Japanese is better than ordinary Japanese people.


When 以上 follows a number, 以上 include the number itself.

日本では20歳以上の人はお酒が飲めます。
- People aged 20 and over can drink alcohol in Japan.

Similarly, 以下 include the number that is followed by it.

日本では19歳以下はお酒が飲めません。
- People aged 19 and under cannot drink alcohol in Japan.

If you don't want to include the number itself, you can use 未満 that means "less than."

日本では20歳未満はお酒が飲めません。
- People who are less than 20 years old cannot drink alcohol in Japan.
(19以下 and 20未満 are the same.)


以上 is also used to mean "now that." A sentence that describes a duty, necessity or something like that follows 以上 in this case.

クビになった以上、新しい仕事を探さないといけない。
- Now that you were fired, you have to find another job.

日本に留学すると決めた以上、日本語を猛勉強しないといけない。
- Now that I've decided to study in Japan, I have to study Japanese hard.

You can use からには instead of 以上 in this case.
クビになったからには、新しい仕事を探さないといけない。
日本に留学すると決めたからには、日本語を猛勉強しないといけない。


"以上" or "以上です," that means "that's all," is used to end your speech or report. You can also say 以上です (That's it) when ordering in a restaurant.

"以上"の説明は以上です。
- That's all my explanation about "以上."
