
Using まだ

When まだ is used in a negative form or with negative word, it means "yet."

まだ宿題が終わってない。
- I haven't finished my homework yet.

彼女がまだ来ない。
- She hasn't come yet.

私はまだ日本語が下手だ。
- I don't have enough skill to speak Japanese yet.


When まだ is used in a positive form, it means "still" or "more."

彼はまだ刑務所にいます。
- He still is in jail.

30才の私の友達はまだ親と一緒に住んでます。
- One of my friend, he is 30 years old, still lives with his parents.

まだ飲みたい。
- I want to drink more.

まだしばらくここにいます。
- I'll stay here for a while.


You can use まだまだ to stress まだ which means "more."

まだまだ日本語の文法を勉強しないといけない。
- I have to study Japanese grammar so much.

意味の分からない日本語の単語がまだまだたくさんあります。
- There are a lot of Japanese words I don't understand the meaning.
