
Using わけではない and わけでもない

わけではない is used when the idea that it follows isn't right.

薬をたくさん飲めば早く良くなるわけではない。
- Taking a lot of medicines doesn't make you feel better faster.

お金を払えば、日本国籍がもらえるわけではない。
- You can't buy Japanese nationality.

高いコンピュータを使えばいいレポートが書けるわけではない。
- Using good computer doesn't mean you can write good reports.


On the other hand, わけでもない is used when the idea is usually right, but sometimes isn't.

高いレストランが美味しい料理を出すというわけでもない。
- Not all expensive restaurants serve you delicious food.

いい選手を集めればチームが強くなるというわけでもない。
- Assembling good players doesn't always make your team stronger.

日本に住めば日本語が上達するというわけでもない。積極的にネイティブに話しかけるべきだ。
- Living in Japan doesn't always make your Japanese better. You need to talk to native speakers actively.


Being rich doesn't mean you are happy.
金持ちは幸せだというわけではない。
金持ちは幸せだというわけでもない。
If you think that being rich has nothing to do with happiness at all, you should say わけではない. If you think that there is a relation between them except a few cases, you should say わけでもない.
