
Using ～といったところ

～といったところ is used to describe a rough number.

駅までどれくらいかかりますか？
歩くと30分、タクシーだと10分といったところです。
- Can you tell me how long it will take to get to the station?
- It takes about 30 minutes by walk, 10 minutes by taxi.

タクシーだといくら位ですか？
2000円といったところだと思います。
- How much do I have to pay for taxi?
- I think it's about 2,000 yen.


It's also used to list examples.

日本のどこに旅行する予定ですか？
東京、京都、大阪といったところです。
- Where are you visiting in Japan?
- Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka and somewhere.
(This answer implies that you don't have a specific itinerary, or you don't want to say all of them specifically.)

あなたの国で人気のあるスポーツは？
サッカーやラグビーといったところです。
- What are popular sports in your country?
- Soccer, rugby or something like that.


It can also follow a clause.

日本語のレベルはどれくらい？
ひらがなとカタカナは読めるけど、漢字はあまり読めないといったところです。
- What's your Japanese level?
- I can read hiragana and katakana, but can't read most kanji. 
(This English answer doesn't have the nuance of といったところです, but I don't know how to say.)


You can say そんなところです by itself to mean "Something like that" or "Sort of; kind of."

その飲み物ってどんな味？ワインみたい？
そんなところ。
- What does that drink taste? Is it like wine?
- Sort of.
