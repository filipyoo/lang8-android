
Using し to Describe Reasons

You can use し to describe reasons, especially when you list two reasons.
You can end your sentence with し in a casual conversation.

日本語を勉強してみたい。漢字はかっこ良く見えるし。
- I'd like to learn Japanese because kanji looks cool.

彼とは話したくない。いばってるし。
- I don't want to talk with him because he's bossy.

彼は絶対この漢字の読み方を知っている。日本人だし。
- I bet he knows how do you read this kanji because he's Japanese.


日本はほんとうに楽しかった。美味しい寿司も食べたし、富士山にも登った。
- I really enjoyed Japan. I had delicious sushi and climbed Mt. Fuji.
The し after the first reason is necessary when you list two reasons, and you can also use し after the second reason:
日本はほんとうに楽しかった。美味しい寿司も食べたし、富士山にも登ったし。

今日は寒いし雨が降ってるから出かけたくない。
- I don't want to go out today because it's cold and raining. 
You can also use し instead of the から, which is used after the second reason, but it might sound slightly unnatural; when you use し before you describe the conclusion, the listener might expect that you are going to list another reason.

彼女は綺麗だし、みんなにやさしいから、学校ですごく人気がある。
- She's very popular in school because she's pretty and nice to everybody.
