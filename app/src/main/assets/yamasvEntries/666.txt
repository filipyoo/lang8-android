
Using そのもの

そのもの means "for itself." It follows a noun. You don't have to use any particles between them.

私は特定の選手やチームというよりもサッカーそのものが好きだ。
- I like soccer for itself rather than specific players or teams.

私は数学そのものには興味が無い。数学を工学や他の分野にどう応用できるかに興味がある。
- I'm not interested in math for itself. I'm interested in how I can apply it to engineering or other fields.

人はお金そのものが好きなわけではありません。なんでも好きなものが買えるからお金が好きなのです。
- People don't like money for itself. They like it because they could buy anything they want if they were rich.


You can also use そのもの to stress characteristics. It follows the stem of a na-adjective in this case.

彼女は親切そのものです。
- She is kindness itself. / She is very kind.

この家は頑丈そのものだ。大きな台風が来ても壊れないだろう。
- This house is very strong. It wouldn't be destroyed by any strong typhoon.

この付近で夜中に一人で外出するのは危険そのものだ。
- Going out at night alone in this area is very dangerous.
