
Common mistakes about 知りません

The following three Japanese sentences sound unnatural.

? 彼に会った時、なんと言っていいか知りません。
I don't know what should I say when I see him.

? この研究をどう進めればいいか知りません。
I don't know how should I push forward with this research.

? 日本語をマスターするのにどの方法が一番いいか知りません。
I don't know which is the best way to master Japanese.

It's unnatural to use 知りません for things which have many answers or solutions. Many people must have various opinions or solutions to those questions: what should you say to him, how should you push forward with this research, and which is the best way to master Japanese. You should say わかりません in these cases.

o 彼に会った時、なんと言っていいかわかりません。
o この研究をどう進めればいいかわかりません。
o 日本語をマスターするのにどの方法が一番いいかわかりません。


On the other hand, you can use both 知りません and わかりません for simple things.

彼女の名前は知りません / わかりません。
I don't know her name.

この漢字の読み方は知りません / わかりません。
I don't know how to read this kanji.

この問題の解き方は知りません / わかりません。
I don't know how to solve this problem.
(You should say わかりません if the problem is difficult. For example: how to replace all pre-existing energy with clean energy. If anyone who knows a simple formula can solve the problem but you don't know it, you can say both 知りません and わかりません.)
