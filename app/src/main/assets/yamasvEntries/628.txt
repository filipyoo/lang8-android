
Using 今日

今日 has two different pronunciations: きょう and こんにち. The former means "today," and the latter means "nowadays." When では follows 今日, it means the latter.

今日(きょう)、スマートフォンを買いました。
- I bought a smartphone today.

今日(こんにち)では、皆がスマートフォンを持ってます。
- Nowadays, everybody has a smartphone.


今日まで can be pronounced as both きょうまで and こんにちまで. If the situation has been continued until today and it changed, きょうまで sounds natural. On the other hand, if something has been continued for a long time till now and it will continue, こんにちまで sounds natural.

今日(きょう）まで、京都を訪れたことはありませんでした。
- I never visited Kyoto until today.

その風習は江戸時代から今日(こんにち)まで続いています。
- This custom continues from Edo era till now.


その病気の原因は今日(きょう)までわかりませんでした。
- The cause of the disease was not identified until today.
This sentence implies that someone identified it today.

その病気の原因は今日(こんにち)までわかっていません。
- The cause of the disease still was not identified.



Some Japanese learner writes 今日は to mean "hello," but it looks weird. "こんにちは" is correct in this case.
