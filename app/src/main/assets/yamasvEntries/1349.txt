
English Phrasal Verbs That Has "off" in Japanese

離陸する - to lift off.

私の乗る飛行機は10時に離陸します。
- My plane will lift off at ten.
(If you say "私の飛行機," it might sound as if you own the plane.)


(手紙を)出す - to send off.

明日その手紙を出します。
- I'll send off the letter tomorrow.
If you are talking about a package, you need to say 送る.


出発する - to head off.

10時に出発します。
- I'll head off at ten.

You can also say 発つ(たつ). It sounds more formal.

今日着いたばかりですが、明日には発たないといけません。
- I've just arrived today, but I have to head off tomorrow.


見送る - to see off.

彼は東京からわざわざ見送りに来てくれた。
- He came to see me off all the way from Tokyo.


笑い飛ばす - to laugh off.

沢山の人が彼のブログにネガティブなコメントを残したが、彼はそれを笑い飛ばした。彼はポジティブなのか馬鹿なのかよくわからない。
- A lot of people left negative comments on his blog, but he just laughed them out. I'm not sure whether he's very positive or stupid.

彼らの批判なんて笑い飛ばせばいい。彼らは単に君の成功を妬んでるだけだ。
- You should laugh off their criticisms.  They were just jealous of your success.
