
Using 山積み

山積み(やまづみ) is used when something is piled up.

机の上に読んでない本が山積みだ。
- A lot of books I haven't read yet are piled up on my desk.

山積みの資料の中から彼は一枚の紙を取り出した。
- He took a piece of paper from beneath a pile of documents.

それはここに山積みされている箱のどれかの中にあるはずだ。
- It should be in one of these box piled up here.


It's also used as a metaphor when you have a lot of things you have to do.

宿題が山積みなので今日は遊びに行けない。
- I have a lot of home work, so I can't hang out today.

これからやることが山積みだ。
- We have a lot of task ahead of us.

新しい大統領は山積みの問題に直面するだろう。
- The new president will face a lot of issues.
