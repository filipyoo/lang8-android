
Using いいですか

"いいですか?" is used to make sure the listener understood what you said.

日本の家に上がる時は靴を脱ぐのを忘れないで下さい。いいですか？
- Don't forget to take off your shoes when entering a Japanese house. Do you understand?
(上がる sounds more natural than 入る when you are talking about a Japanese house.)


It's also used to attract the listener's attention before you say something important.

いいですか？　日本の家に上がる時は靴を脱ぐのを忘れないで下さい。
- Listen. Don't forget to take off your shoes when entering a Japanese house.


You can say simply "いい?" in a casual conversation.

年上の人に話しかける時は名前の後に「さん」をつけたほうがいいよ。いい？
- You should add "san" after their name when you talk to someone older than you. Understand?

いい？これは私のノートPCなんだから、勝手に使わないで。
- Look! This is my own laptop. Don't use it without asking.
