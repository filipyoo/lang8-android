
Using どうせ

どうせ means "anyway." It's often used in negative sentences.

どうせ私を好きな人なんかいない。
- No one likes me anyway.

一生懸命勉強してもどうせその試験には受からない。
- I won't be able to pass the exam anyway, even if I study hard.


どうせ〜なら means "if you do something." 

どうせ東京の寿司屋に行くなら、一番高い店に行きたい。
- If I go to a sushi restaurant in Tokyo, I'd like to go to the most expensive one.

どうせ大阪と京都に旅行するなら、近いから奈良にも行った方がいい。
- If you visit Osaka and Kyoto, you should also visit Nara. It' very close to there. 


You can omit the clause between どうせ and なら when the action is implied.

いつか東京の寿司屋に行きたい。どうせなら一番高い店に行きたい。
- I'd like to go to a sushi restaurant in Tokyo someday. If I do, I'd like to go to the most expensive one.

今週末、大阪と京都に旅行するんだ。
どうせなら、近いから奈良にも行った方がいい。
- I'm going to Osaka and Kyoto this weekend.
- If you are, you should also visit Nara. It' s very close to there.
