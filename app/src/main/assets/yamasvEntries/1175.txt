
Using 差し当たり

差し当たり(さしあたり) means "for now / at present." It's used to say what you need at present.

お金を貸してくれる？
いくら？
差し当たり、5000円くらい。
- Can I borrow some money from you?
- How much?
- About five thousand yen for now.
The last Japanese sentence implies that they might ask for more money soon.

会社を興す予定だけど事務所を借りる金がないから、差し当たりはガレージで仕事するつもりだ。
- I am starting my business, but I don't have money enough to rent an office. Therefore, I'll work in my garage for the moment.

いいアパートを見つけたけど、家具がまだ何もない。差し当たり、ベッドとテーブルと椅子を買わなきゃ。
- I found a nice flat, but there is no furniture yet. I need to buy a bed, table, and chairs for now.

日本に行くから、差し当たり、"Excuse me"と"I'm sorry"と”Thank you"を日本語でなんて言うか覚えなきゃ。
だったら、"すみません"だけ覚えればいい。全部の意味で使えるから。
- I'm visiting Japan. I need to learn how to say "Excuse me," "I'm sorry" and "Thank you" in Japanese at first.
- Then, all you need to learn is "すみません." It means all of them.
