
Using 出てくる

出てくる means "to get out."

彼がやっとトイレから出てきた。
- He finally got out of the bathroom.

彼は日曜日には昼ごろまでベッドから出てこない。
- He doesn't get out of the bed until about noon on Sundays.


It's also used to mean "to appear" in movies, anime or something like that.

この俳優はギャング映画にはいつも出てくる。
- This actor always appears in gangster movies.

東京の女子高生はみんなアニメに出てくるようなミニスカートをはいている。
- Every Japanese high school girl in Tokyo wears a miniskirt like in anime.

ミッションインポッシブル5に出てくるバイクが欲しい。
- I want the motorcycle that appears in Mission Impossible 5.


ちょっと出てくる is a common phrase that means "I'll go out for a while." It implies you'll come back soon.


出てくる is also used to mean "to come up with."

常識にとらわれていてはいいアイデアは出てこない。
- You won't come up with a good idea if you are caught by common sense.


出てこない is also used when you can't remember something.

彼の名前が出てこない。
- I can't remember his name.

日本語で「thank you」が出てこない。
- I can't remember how do I say "thank you" in Japanese.
