
The usage of からこそ.

からこそ and から have the same meaning "because." They are used to describe reasons. However, からこそ is used to stress the reason.

私は日本で働きたい からこそ、日本語を一生懸命勉強している.
I am studying  Japanese hard, especially because I want to work in Japan.

あなたが手伝ってくれた からこそ、試験に合格できました.
I've passed the exam, especially because of all your help. 

あなたがいた からこそ、試合に勝てた.
We could win the game only because of you.
We couldn't win the game without you.

あなたに才能があると思っている からこそ、いつも厳しく評価するのです。 
It's precisely because they think you are a gifted person that they always judge you strictly.


You can use こその as an adjective which is used to stress the reason. "No music, No life." can be translated to "音楽あって こその 人生."

あなたがいて こその 私の人生.
(あなたのいない私の人生なんて想像もできない.)
I can't even imagine life without you.

苦しみあって こその 喜び.
(苦しみがなければ、楽しみもない.)
If there were no clouds, we should not enjoy the sun. [Proverb]
