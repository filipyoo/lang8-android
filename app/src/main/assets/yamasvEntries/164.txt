
〜うちに / 〜あいだに

〜うちに means "while."

明るい うちに 帰りなさい - Go home while it is still light.
晴れている うちに 帰りなさい - Go home while it's not yet raining.
学生のうちに英語をマスターしなさい - You need to master the English while you are a student.

It can come after a negative form, it means "before" in this case.

暗くならない うちに 帰りなさい- Go home before it gets dark.  
雨が降らない うちに 帰りなさい - Go home before it starts to rain.

〜うちに implies that the status will change, so you can alter a sentence which is used うちに by using までに.
暗くなる までに 帰りなさい。
雨が降りだす までに 帰りなさい。
卒業 までに 英語をマスターしなさい。


あいだに is a similar word to うちに. "学生の うちに 英語をマスターしなさい" and "学生の あいだに 英語をマスターしなさい" have the same meanings if the listener is a student.
However, あいだに is used when the beginning is clear. On the other hand, うちに focuses only the end.
You can say "夏休みの あいだに、この本を読みたい" when your summer vacation has not started yet, but it sounds unnatural to say "夏休みの うちに、この本を読みたい" if your summer vacation doesn't start yet. "夏休みの うちに、この本を読みたい" implies that your summer vacation has already started.
Similarly, "暗くならない あいだに うちに帰りなさい" sounds unnatural because you are not usually aware of when the sun rises today. On the other hand, "雨がやんでいる あいだに うちに帰りなさい" sounds natural if you are aware of when the rain was stopped and you think it will start to rain again.
