
How to Say "That means 〜" in Japanese


You can say つまり or ということは to say "That means." It's used you describe a conclusion judging from the fact which you mentioned in the sentence before it.

3期以上アメリカの大統領を務めることはできない。つまり/ということは オバマは次の大統領選には出れない。
- You can't serve as the president of the United States for more than two terms. That means Obama can't stand for it again.

円安が進んでいる。つまり/ということは 前より安い値段で日本に行ける(ということだ)。
- Japanese Yen is getting weaker against US dollars. That means you can go to Japan for cheaper prices than before. 

日本ではアメリカドルは使えない。つまり/ということは 日本で買い物したければ円に両替しないといけない。
- You can't use US dollars in Japan. That means you have to exchange your's to Japanese Yen if you want to buy something there.
