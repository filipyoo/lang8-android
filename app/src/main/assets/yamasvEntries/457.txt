
Indirect Speech in Japanese

Unlike English, you don't have to change the tense in indirect speech.

彼女は、「雨が降っている」と言った - She told me "It's raining."
彼女は、雨が降っていると言った - She told me that It was raining.
If you say "彼女は雨が降っていたといった," it means that it had been raining before then but stopped.

彼は、「ちょうど今着いた」と言った。- He said "I've just arrived."
彼は、ちょうど今着いたと言った。- He said that he had just arrived.


Of course, you have to change the subject and the object in some cases.

彼は、「君が好きだ」と言った。- He said "I like you" to me.
彼は、私が好きだと言った。 - He said that he liked me.
(Similarly, if you say "彼は、私が好きだったと言った," it means that he didn't like you then.)


Unfortunately, there is a little complicated rule.

彼は、「君に千円あげる」と言った。- He said "I'll give you one thousand yen."

"彼は、私に千円あげると言った" isn't a correct indirect speech form of the Japanese sentence. You have to say "彼は、私に千円'くれる'と言った." Since くれる/あげる have a direction, you have to change the word.

彼は、「手伝ってあげる」と言った。- He said that "I'll help you."
彼は、手伝ってくれると言った。- He said that he would help me.
