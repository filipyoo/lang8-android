
The Usage of 最大〜 and 最低〜


最大 is used to show the maximum number of length, weight, etc.

追加料金を払えば、この製品の保証期間を最大3年延長できます。
- You can expand the length of warranty of this product for up to three years by paying extra money.

この電車は最大700人乗せることができます。
- This train can bring up to 700 passengers. 


最大でも is used when you want to stress that it's impossible to over the number. It's often used with negative sentences.

私は昼食には最大でも500円しか払いません。
- I can only pay 500 yen at most for lunch. 

このバスは最大でも80人しか乗れません。
- This bus can't bring over 80 people at the same time.


On the other hand, 最低 is used to show the minimum number.

その資格をとるには、この講習を最低30時間受けることが必要です。
- You have to take this course at least 30 hours to get the license.

この城を買うには最低でも20億円用意しないといけません。
- You have to prepare at least two billions yen to buy this castle.
