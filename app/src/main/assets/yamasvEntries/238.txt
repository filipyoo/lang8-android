
The Usage of 〜で言うところの〜

〜で言うところの〜 is used to explain something by using other words so that the listener can imagine the meanings easily.

When I explain Yomiuri Giants to American people who knows baseball in America well but are not familiar with that in Japan, I would say:
読売ジャイアンツはアメリカ で言うところの ニューヨークヤンキースです。
(Yomiuri Giants in Japan are like NewYork Yankees in America.)

By explaining like this, they would know that Yomiuri Giants are a very popular and powerful baseball team in Japan.


日本のセンター試験というのは、米国 で言うところの SATのようなものです。
"センター試験" in Japan is like SAT in America.

コンピュータのCPUは、人間 で言うところの 脳に相当します。
The CPU of a computer is basically equivalent to the brain of a human being.

ヨドバシカメラは、米国 で言うところの Best Buyのような家電量販店です。
ヨドバシカメラ is a consumer electronics corporation, which is like Best Buy in America.
