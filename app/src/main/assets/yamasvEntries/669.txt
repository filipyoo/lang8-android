
Using 追い追い(おいおい)

おいおい means "step by step" or "after a while." It's usually written by hiragana.

When it means "step by step," You can also use 徐々に instead.

4週間で全部の平仮名と漢字を覚えました。おいおい/徐々に 漢字も覚えていくつもりです。
- I've learned all hiragana and kanji in the last four weeks. I'm going to learn kanji too step by step.

フライトレコーダーが見つかったので、その飛行機事故の原因は、おいおい/徐々に判るだろう。
- Since the flight recorder was found, the cause of the plane crash will be disclosed step by step.


When it means "after a while." You can also use そのうち instead.

この頃すごく暑いけど、おいおい/そのうち 涼しくなっていくでしょう。
- It's very hot lately, but it will be cooler after a while.

彼は若い時に成功したから人生は簡単だと思ってる。でも、おいおい/そのうち そうでないとわかるだろう。
- He thinks that life is easy because he has succeeded when he is young. However, after a while, he must learn that it isn't.


It sounds unnatural to use おいおい to describe past events.

日本語の会話力が、徐々に(xおいおい) 上がってきた。
- My Japanese conversation skills have improved step by step.
