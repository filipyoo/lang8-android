
Using とした and てみた

とした means "to try to do." It follows the volitional form of verbs. 

寿司を作ろうとしたけど、お酢が足りないと気づいて諦めた。
- We tried to make sushi, but gave up because we realized that we don't have enough vinegar to make.

倉庫のドアを開けようとしたけど、鍵がかかっていた。
- I tried to open the door of the warehouse, but it was locked.


てみた means "to try doing." 

寿司を作ってみたけど、東京で食べたのと全然違った。
- We tried making sushi, but it was totally different from what we had had in Tokyo.

倉庫のドアを開けてみたけど、中は空っぽだった。
- I tried opening the door of the warehouse, but there was nothing in it.


やってみる is a natural expression to mean "I'll try." It can be used for any verb.

向こう岸まで泳げると思う?
やってみる。
- Do you think you can swim to the opposite bank?
- I'll try.

このケーキ１人で丸ごと食べられると思う?
やってみる。
- Do you think you can eat this whole cake by yourself?
- I'll try.
