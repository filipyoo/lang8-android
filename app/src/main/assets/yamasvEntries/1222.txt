
Using 〜たら〜たで

〜たら〜たで is used when an action would bring you good and bad effects at the same time. 

孫に会いたいけど、いたらいたでうるさい。
- I miss my grandson, but if he were here, he would be noisy.

あの高い自転車を買いたいけど、買ったら買ったでいつも盗まれないように注意しないといけない。
- I want to get that expensive bike, but if I bought that, I would always have to pay attention not to be stolen.

試合に負けたら負けたで何か学べるはずだ。
- Even if you wouldn't win a match, you would learn something from it. 

お腹が空いていると集中できないけど、食べたら食べたで眠くなる。
- I can't concentrate when I'm hungry, but if I ate something, it could make me sleepy.
