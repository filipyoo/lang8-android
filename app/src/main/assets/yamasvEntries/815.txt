
Using 高く/安く つく

つく can be used to mean "to cost" when it follows 高く/安く.

飛行機で行くと高くつくから、新幹線で行った方がいい。
You should go by Shinkansen. It'll cost you more to go by plane.

毎日外で食べると高くつくから自炊することにした。
I've decided to cook my own meal because it would be more expensive to eat out every day.

高い服は長く着られるので、結局は安くつく。
It's cheaper in the end to buy expensive clothes because you can wear them for a long time.
