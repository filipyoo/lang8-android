
Using たかだか and せいぜい

When たかだか(高々) and せいぜい are followed by some number, they are used to stress that the price, time or something like that is very low/short.

この工場で一日中働いても、たかだか/せいぜい 3000円しかもらえない。
- You can get 3,000 yen at most even if you work all day in this factory.

このレストランで、何をお腹いっぱい食べても　一人あたり たかだか/せいぜい 2000円くらいです。
- It wouldn't over 2,000 yen a head even if you eat any dishes until you are filled in this restaurant.

駅まで たかだか/せいぜい 300メートルくらいだから歩いた方がいい。
- It's only about 300 meters to the station. You should walk.

まだ たかだか/せいぜい 10分くらいしか走ってない。
- I've been running for only about 10 minutes.


The opposite expression of them are 少なくとも/少なくても (at least.) The particle は follows a number when you use it. 少なくとも sounds slightly more formal than 少なくても.

この工場で働ければ、一日で少なくとも10,000円はもらえる。
- If you were hired by this factory, you could make 10,000 yen a day at least.

その寿司屋でお腹いっぱい食べたら少なくとも20,000円はかかる。
- You have to pay over 20,000 yen if you eat sushi until you are filled in the sushi restaurant.
(You can also use 安くても instead in this sentence.)

駅まで 少なくとも3キロはあるからタクシーで行った方がいい。
- It's about at least 3 kilometers to the station. You should take a taxi.
