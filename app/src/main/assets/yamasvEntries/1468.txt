
Using よろしく

よろしく means "nice to meet you." 

ケンです。よろしくお願いします。
- Nice to meet you. My name is Ken.
You can omit お願いします in a casual situation.


It's also used when you ask someone for a favor.

誰か私の文章を添削してくれませんか。よろしくお願いします。
- Can someone correct my sentence. Thank you.
You can omit お願いします in a casual situation too.


〜によろしく means "Say hello to 〜."

レイチェルによろしく。
- Say hello to Rachel.
In this case, you can't add お願いします after よろしく. If you want to say it more politely, you can say よろしくお伝え下さい.


When 〜をよろしく follows a person, it means "Take care of 〜."

赤ちゃんをよろしく。
- (To a sitter:) Take care of our baby.


〜をよろしく is also used to remind somebody of something.

金曜日までにレポートをよろしく。
- Make sure to finish the report by Friday.
