
Using なんだかんだ言って

なんだかんだ言って means "anyway" or "in the end." It's used when you do something after complaining about it or when you know that there are different opinions.

なんだかんだ言って彼は全額支払った。
- He paid all the money in the end after complaining a lot.

(彼は奥さんの悪口ばかり言うけど)なんだかんだ言って彼は奥さんを愛してるって皆知っている。
- He always says bad things about his wife, but everybody knows that he loves her.

(日本車は退屈だしかっこよくないと言う人もいるけど)なんだかんだ言って日本車は信頼性が高いからよく売れている。
- Some people say that Japanese cars are boring or they aren't cool, but a lot of people buy them because they are reliable.

(日本語はむずかしいし英語みたいにポピュラーじゃないけど)なんだかんだ言って私は日本語が好きだ。
- Japanese is very difficult and not popular as English, but I like it anyway.

You can omit the phrase in the parenthesis in each sentence above because なんだかんだ言って implies it.
