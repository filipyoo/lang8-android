
The volitional form of verbs + にも.

にも follows what you want to do, and it is followed by the reason you can't do it. 

外出しよう にも、雨が降っている (だから外出できない)。
I want to go out, but it's raining. (Therefore I can't go out.)
(だから外出できない is omitted in this sentence; you don't have to say that because the listener can easily guess the sentence.)

車を買おう にも、お金がない。
I want to buy a car, but I don't have enough money.

働こう にも、仕事がない。
I want to work, but there is no job.

彼に電話しよう にも、電話番号がわからない。
I want to call him, but I don't know his phone number.


This expression is not often used in casual conversation, "たい-form + けど" sounds more natural when you are talking with your friends.

外出したいけど、雨が降っている。
車を買いたいけど、お金がない。
働きたいけど、仕事がない。
彼に電話したいけど、電話番号がわからない。
