
The Usage of 〜だけの

When だけの comes after the dictionary form or the ta-form of verbs, it means "something necessary to ..." or "something corresponding to ...."
Both negative and positive forms can come after it.


あの車を買う だけの お金はない.
I don't have enough money to buy that car.

10台の車を駐車する だけの スペースはない.
There is not enough space to park ten cars. 

この家には、20人が泊まれる だけの 部屋があります。
There are enough rooms to hold twenty people in this house.

彼は、この店の商品を全て買う だけの お金を持っています。
He has enough money to buy all the goods in this shop.

あの映画には金を払った だけの 価値がなかった。
That movie wasn't worth what I paid for it.
