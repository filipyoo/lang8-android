
"のために" may sound unnatural.

You may have learned that "〜のために" means "for," but saying のために to mean "for" sounds unnatural in some cases. に sounds natural to mean "for" in the following sentences.

1. 私は毎日娘に弁当を作る。
I make a bento for my daughter every day.

2. 彼らに何をしてあげればいいだろう。
What should I do for them?

3. 朝食にトーストを食べた。
I ate toast for breakfast.

4. おみやげに箸を買った。
I bought chopsticks for souvenirs.

5. 面接にスーツを着るべきかわからない。
I'm not sure if I should wear a suit for my job interview.

6. それは葬式に適した服ではありません。
They are not appropriate clothes for a funeral.

7. 私は時々、気分転換に海に行く。
I sometimes go to the sea for recreation.

In the sentence 1 and 2, in which "for" is followed by people, "のために" is acceptable, but the rest of the sentences sound unnatural if you use のために instead of に.
