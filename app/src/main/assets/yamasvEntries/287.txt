
The Usage of 〜っけ

〜っけ is a casual expression and is added at the end of a question form. It's used when you forget something.

今日は何曜日だ っけ？
今日は何曜日ですか？ (polite)
What day is it today?

彼の名前は何だ っけ？
彼の名前は何でしたか？ (polite)
(I forget his name.) Can you tell me his name?
You can use this expression when you forget his name, You can't say it when you don't know his name from the beginning.

彼は誰だ っけ？
彼は誰でしたか？ (polite)
(I've seen him, but I can't remember who he is.) Can you tell me who he is?

結納ってどういう意味だっけ？
結納ってどういう意味でしたか？ (polite)
(I've learned the word, but I forget it.) What does 結納 mean?


You can also use it to confirm something.

今日は金曜日だっけ?
今日は金曜日ですよね? (polite)
It's Friday, isn't it?

彼女の誕生日って、今度の金曜日だっけ？
彼女の誕生日って、今度の金曜日ですよね？ (polite)
Her birthday is next Friday. Is it right?


You can also use it when you are looking back on past events.

よくこの公園で野球して遊んだっけ。
We used to play baseball in this park together.

子供の頃いつもあなたから日本語を教えてもらったっけ。
You always taught me Japanese when I was a child.


It's usually used in casual conversation, but it can come after a polite form. 

今日は何曜日でした っけ？
結納ってどういう意味でしたっけ？
今日は金曜日でしたっけ?
よくこの公園で野球して遊ましたっけ。
子供の頃いつもあなたから日本語を教えてもらいましたっけ。
