
Using ずば抜けて/ダントツで

すば抜けて/ダントツで means "by far the most 〜." The latter sounds a little more casual.

彼の給料はプロのサッカー選手の中でも すば抜けて/ダントツで 高い。
- His salary is by far the highest among all professional soccer players.

この店の寿司は すば抜けて/ダントツで 美味しい。
- The sushi at this restaurant is by far the most delicious.


Then can't be used to mean "by far more 〜." You should say はるかに when you are comparing two things or people.

彼の日本語は私よりはるかに上手い。
- His Japanese is by far better than mine.

日本の寿司は私の国のものよりはるかに美味しい。
- Sushi in Japan is by far better than that in my country.
