
How To Say "I'm worried I 〜" In Japanese

You can say 〜か心配. 試験をパスするか心配 or 試験に失敗しないか失敗 means "I'm worried I won't pass the exam." か心配 always follows a good result for you. (試験をパスする or 試験に失敗しない is a good result for you.) If you say 試験にパスしないか失敗, it means that you want to fail the exam. This rule is completely opposite from English; a bad result follows "I'm worried" in English.

妊娠してないか心配。
- I'm worried I'm pregnant.
(The speaker doesn't want to be pregnant. She wants the result "I'm not pregnant(妊娠してない)."

金曜日までに終わるか心配。
- I'm worried I won't be able to finish it by Friday.

彼らが私を好きか心配。
= 彼らが嫌っていないか心配。
- I'm worried they don't like me.

同じような事故がまた起きないか心配。
- I'm worried a similar accident will happen again.

日本語を話せるようになるか心配。
- I'm worried I won't be able to speak Japanese.
