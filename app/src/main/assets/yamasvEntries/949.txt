
Using 的(まと)

的 means the target of darts, archery, etc.

あの的に3回続けて当てられたら1000円あげるよ。
- If you can hit the target 3 times in a row, I'll give you 1,000 yen.


的 is also used as a metaphor for "the point" or "the essence." 的を射る originally means to hit the Japanese archery (弓道) target, but it's usually used to mean "to hit the head of the nail."

彼の言ったことは的を射ている。私達は計画を見なおした方がいい。
- What he said hit the head of the nail. We should reconsider our plan.

この分析は的を射ている。
- The analysis is right to the point.


的外れ is a noun which means "off/beside the point."

それは的はずれだ。
- That's off the point.

彼の主張は的外れだ。何か勘違いをしてるんだろう。
- His claim is beside the point. I think he's misunderstanding something.


注目の的 means the center of attention or to be highlighted.

宝くじに当たってから、彼は注目の的だ。
- He has attracted a lot of public attention since he won the lottery.


非難の的 means the target of criticism.

彼はTVで貧しい人たちを馬鹿にしたので、非難の的になっている。
- Since he made fun of poor people on TV, he becomes the target of criticism
