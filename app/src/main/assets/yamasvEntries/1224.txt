
Using 〜だの〜だの

だの can be used to list nouns you don't know or you don't like. It's used in a negative sentence.

私の１７才の孫は、ナルトだのワンピースだのマンガばかり読んでいる。私達が17才の頃はマンガなんて読まなかった。あんなのは小さい子供の読み物だ。
- My grandson, who is 17 years old, is always reading cartoons, such as Naruto or One Piece. When we were in his age, we didn't read cartoons. Only little kids read them.
(This sentence implies that the speaker don't like the fact that 17-years-old boy is reading cartoons.)

ここはアメリカなのに通りを走ってる車はトヨタだのホンダだのフォルクスワーゲンだの輸入車ばっかりだ。気に入らない。
- This is America, but almost all the cars passing on the street are imported: Toyota, Honda, or Volkswagen. I don't like it!

初めてテレビでラグビーを見たけど、スクラムだのモールだのノックオンだの知らない言葉ばかりで全く楽しめなかった。
- I watched a rugby match for the first time on TV, but the reporter was saying a lot of unfamiliar words, such as scrum, maul, or knock on. I couldn't enjoy it at all.


It can also follow a clause.

旅行の間、彼はずっと「料理が辛すぎる」だの「部屋が狭すぎる」だの文句ばかり言っていた。おかげでこっちも楽しくなかった。
- During the journey, he was complaining all the time, such as "The dishes is too spicy" or "This room is too small." It made us uncomfortable.

うちの会社の規則は、厳しすぎる。「髪の毛を染めたらいけない」だの「ダークスーツを着ないといけない」だの。
- Our company's rules are a little too strict, such as "You can't color your hair" or "You have to wear a dark suit."
