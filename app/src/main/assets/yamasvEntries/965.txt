
I'm Using さっぱり

"さっぱり〜ない" means "not at all." さっぱり is always used with a negative form.

この数学の公式が何を意味するかさっぱりわからない。
- I completely can't understand what this math formula means.

この国ではドイツ車はよく売れるが、日本車はさっぱり売れない。
- German cars are sold well in this country, but Japanese cars aren't at all.


You can use it by itself.

（釣りをしている人に) 魚はたくさん釣れましたか?
さっぱりです。
- (To someone who is fishing.) Did you catch a lot of fish?
- Not at all.

なんで彼が怒ってるのか知ってる?
さっぱり。
- Do you know why he's angry?
- I have no idea.


さっぱり is also used to describe light taste.

ラーメンと違って、蕎麦はさっぱりした味です。
- Unlike ramen, soba has light taste.

The opposite of さっぱり is こってり(rich) / しつこい(heavy). しつこい has a negative nuance.
