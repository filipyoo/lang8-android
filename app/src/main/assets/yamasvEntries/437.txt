
The Usage of 大変

大変 is used as both a na-adjective and a adverb.

When it's used as na-adjectives, it means "difficult / hard."  

敬語をマスターするのは大変です。
- It's very difficult to master keigo.

消防士は大変な仕事です。
消防士の仕事は大変です。
- Firefighting is a very hard job. 

Since 大変 has a nuance of "to have a trouble," "大変な人" means "a person who has a trouble." 

彼は借金をたくさんかかえて大変です。
( = 彼は借金をたくさんかかえて困っている)
- He has a lot of trouble; he's deeply in debt.

友人は仕事を首になって大変です。
( = 友人は仕事を首になって困っている)
My friend has a trouble because he was fired.


大変 can also be used to mean "serious."

ハリケーンは大変な被害をこの地域に与えた。
- The hurricane caused serious damage to this area.

父は大変な病気にかかってます。
- My father has a serious disease.

It sounds a little unnatural to use 大変 as the predicate in this case.
? ハリケーンがこの地域に与えた被害は大変です。
? 父の病気は大変です。
Using 深刻 sounds more natural in this case.
o ハリケーンがこの地域に与えた被害は深刻です。
o 父の病気は深刻です。


When 大変 is used as a adverb, it means "very." Young people might not often use 大変 to mean "very."

桜は大変綺麗です - Cherry blossom is a very beautiful.
この料理は大変美味しいです- This dish is very delicious.
