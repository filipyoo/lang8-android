
Causative Expressions In Japanese

When you make someone or let someone do something, you can say 〜せる. 

疲れていたので彼に運転させた。
- I made him drive my car because I was tired.

運転したがったので、彼に運転させた。
- I let him drive my car because he wanted to.


You can also say 〜せる when you have someone or get someone to do something. However, it sounds bossy. Most Japanese people would say "してもらった." 

そのスタイリストに髪を切らせた。(It's correct, but sounds bossy.)
そのスタイリストに髪を切ってもらった。
- I had the hair stylist cut my hair.

私はその先生にその動詞の使い方を説明してもらった。
- I had my teacher explain the usage of the verb.
(You may have the right to have the teacher explain, but 説明させた sounds very rude, you mustn't say 〜せる for someone you should respect.)

妹に私の部屋を掃除させた。(It's correct, but sounds bossy.)
妹に私の部屋を掃除してもらった。
- I got my sister to clean my room.
