
The Usage of のに

のに is used to mean "but / although."

先月日本に行ったのに、ほとんど毎日雨だった.
- We visited Japan last month, but we had rain almost every day.

がんばったのに、期限までに間に合わなかった。
- We couldn't finish the job despite our hard work.


のに also can be used to mean "in order to." It comes after the u-form of verbs in this case.

この計画を成功させるのに、あなたの助けが必要です。
- Your help is needed to make our plan a success.

学校に行くのに、自転車を使っています。
- I went to school by bicycle.

漢字を勉強するのに、このアプリが便利ですよ。
- This app is very useful to study kanji.

私は老眼なので、新聞を読むのに老眼鏡が必要です。
- I need reading glasses when I read a newspaper because I have aged eyes.

早く走るのに、正しい腕のふりが重要です。
Proper arm swing is important to run faster.


日本に行くのに、お金が足りない。
This Japanese sentence can be read in two meanings.
1. I'm going to Japan, but I don't have enough money. (日本に行く予定だけど、お金が足りない)
2. I don't have money enough to go to Japan. (日本に行くためのお金がない)
