
Using 実に

実に(じつに) means "very," which is used to stress an adjective.

この本は実におもしろい。
- This book is very interesting.

実に多くの外国人が京都を訪れる。
- A very large number of foreigners visit Kyoto.

It can also stress a number that follows it.

実に1億人がその試合をテレビで見たと言われている。
- Surprisingly, it is said that a hundred million people watched the game on TV.

この大学の卒業生の実に80人以上がノーベル賞を受賞している。
- Surprisingly, over 80 people who graduated from this university won the Nobel Prizes.

彼は実に10年以上かけてその本を書いた。
- It took over ten long years for him to write the book.
