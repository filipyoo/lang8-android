
How To Say "I'm coming" In Japanese

When you say 行く or 来る, you should chose a verb from the point of speaker's view (not listener's view.) Therefore, when someone knocks a door and you want to say "I'm coming," you should say "今、行きます." "今、来ます" is incorrect.

私の誕生日パーティに来てくれますか? - Will you come to my birthday party? 
はい、行きます。- Yes, I will (come.) 
("はい、来ます" is incorrect.)

A: パーティに来る? - Will you come to the party?
B: 彼女は来るの? - Will she come?
A: 来るよ - Yes, she will.
B: じゃあ、僕も行く - Then, I'll come too.

すぐそこに行きます。- I'll be there soon. 
("すぐそこに来ます" or "すぐそこにいます" is incorrect.)

3時に行きます - I'll be there at three.

今、向かっています。 - I'm on my way.
