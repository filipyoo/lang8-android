
Using 気分

気分が悪い has two meanings: "I feel sick" and "to be disgusted." 気分がいい means "I feel good (not sick,)" and also means "I feel great."

気分が悪い。風邪をひいたかも。
- I feel sick, I might catch a cold.

この薬を飲んだら気分が良くなった。
- This medicine made me feel better.

給料を減らされて気分が悪い。
- I'm disgusted because they cut my pay.

ムカつく上司がクビになったから気分がいい。
- I feel great because my disgusting boss was fired.


When you are talking about someone else, 機嫌 sounds more natural.

私の上司はいつも機嫌が悪いけど、今日は機嫌がいい。何があったんだろう?
- My boss is always in a bad mood, but is in a good mood today. What happened to him? 

If you say "彼は気分が悪そうだ," it usually means "he looks sick."


〜たい気分 means "in a mood for 〜."

今日は飲みたい気分じゃない。
- I'm not in a mood for drinking.

今夜はワインを飲みたい気分だ。
- I'm in a mood for wine tonight.
今夜はワインの気分だ is acceptable, but some people may say that it's incorrect.

踊りたい気分だ。
- I'm in a mood for dancing.


気分屋 means "a moody person."

彼は気分屋だ。
- He's moody.
