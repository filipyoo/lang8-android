
Using ただ

ただ is an adverb that means "just."

ただ見てるだけです。
- ( In a shop:) Just looking.

ただ挨拶に寄っただけです。
- I just stopped by to say hi.

ただ車に触っただけで、彼はすごく怒った。
- He got mad when I just touched his car.


When it modifies a noun, you need to say ただの.

私はただの客です。店員ではありません。
- I'm just a customer, not a clerk of this shop.

彼はただの従業員なのに、社長のようにえらそうだ。
- Although he's just an employee, he acts bossy as if he's the president.


ただ is also a noun that means "free."

このダンボール箱はただです。好きなだけ持っていってください。
- These cardboard boxes are free. You can take them as many as you want.

ただで means "for free."

このレストランで働けば、毎日晩御飯がただで食べられる。
- If you work in this restaurant, you can have dinner every day for free.


ただ is also used as a conjunction that means "but." It's a short form of ただし.

日本のタクシーはとてもきれいでドライバーは礼儀正しい。ただ、料金が高い。
- Taxis in Japan are very clean and the drivers are very polite, but expensive.

このホテルはすごく居心地が良くて料金も高くない。ただ、空港から遠すぎる。
- This hotel is very comfortable and not expensive, but it's too far from the airport.
