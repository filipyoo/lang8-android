
しか and だけ

しか and だけ are used to describe the word "only," but they are not interchangeable.


しか is always followed by a negative form.

100円しか持ってない.
I have only one hundred yen.

太郎しか来ていない.
Only Taro has come.



だけ can be followed by a negative and positive form.

太郎だけ、来た.  ( = 太郎しか、来ていない.)
Only Taro has come. (Nobody but Taro has not come yet.)

太郎だけ、来ていない. ( = 太郎以外は、来た.)
Only Taro has not come yet. (Everybody but Taro has come.)


100円しか持っていない and 100円だけ持っている have the same meaning but the former gives you the impression that the speaker is disappointed at the situation.


You can use them together. You can say 100円だけしか持っていない. It has the same meaning as 100円しか持っていない, but だけしか gives you the impression that "only" is stressed.
You can't say しかだけ.


You can place しか after another particle, and you can place だけ before and after another particle:
私は新しい住所を、太郎 に しか 教えていない。
= 私は太郎 に だけ 新しい住所を教えた。
= 私は太郎 だけ に 新しい住所を教えた。
I told only Taro my new address.


You can use のみ instead of だけ. のみ is mainly used in written language.
太郎のみ、来た.
太郎のみ、来ていない.
