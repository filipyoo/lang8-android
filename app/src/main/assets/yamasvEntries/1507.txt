
Using ～なんてもんじゃない

Although なんてもんじゃない end with negative form, ない, it's used to stress an adjective it follows.

この料理はおいしいなんてもんじゃない。
- This dish is extremely delicious. /  "Delicious" is an understatement.
It implies that you don't know a proper expression that describes how delicious the dish is.

この料理はまずいなんてもんじゃない。豚も食べないだろう。
- This dish is extremely awful. Even a pig wouldn't eat them.

今日は寒いね。
寒いなんてもんじゃない。
- It's cold today, isn't it?
- I feel like I'm in a fridge.


It can also follow a noun or verb.

彼は優秀なんてもんじゃない。正真正銘の天才だ。
- "Excellent" is a modest expression to describe him. He's a real genius.

この記事は間違いなんてもんじゃない。作者は全く取材してないんだろう。
- This article is completely wrong. The writer mustn't have covered it at all.

昨夜は飲んだの？
飲んだなんてもんじゃない。
- Did you drink last night?
- We drunk like a fish. 

お腹すいた?
すいたなんてもんじゃない。
- Are you hungry?
- I could eat an elephant.
