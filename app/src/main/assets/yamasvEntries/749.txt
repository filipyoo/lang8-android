
How to Say "take/bring" in Japanese

There is no difference between "take" and "bring" in Japanese when you say て行く. However, you need to use "連れて行く" for living things. Otherwise, you need to use "持っていく."

あなたの家にワインを一本持って行きます。
I'll bring a bottle of wine to your house.

あなたの家からワインを一本持って行きます。
I'll take a bottle of wine from your house.

あなたの家に子供を連れて行きます。
I'll bring my kids to your house.

公園に子供を連れて行きます。
I'll take my kids to the park.


連れて行く and 連れて来る are different. It depends on where the speaker is. 来る is used when the subject comes where the speaker is. 行く is used when the subject goes where the speaker isn't.

学校に行って子供を公園に連れて行きます。
I'm going to the school and take my kids to the park.

学校に行って子供をここに連れて来ます。
I'm going to the school and bring my kids here.

彼女は子供を私の家に連れて来た。
She brought her kids to my house.

彼女は子供を学校に連れて行った。
She took her kids to the school.

彼女は子供を学校に連れて来た。
She brought her kids to the school.
(This implies that the speaker was at the school.)
