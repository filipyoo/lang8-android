
The Usage of "〜というのに"

"〜というのに" means "despite / while / even." It follows a noun or a sentence. When it follows a noun, you have to add だ after the noun.

4月だというのにすごく寒い。
- Even though it's April now, it's very cold.

30分前に昼ごはんを食べたというのに、お腹がすいた。
- I ate lunch 30 minutes ago, but I'm hungry.

彼は警察官だというのに、時々飲酒運転をする。
- Despite he is a police officer, he sometimes drinks and drives.

一年間日本語を勉強したというのに、先月日本に行った時、日本人の言ってることがほとんどわからなかった。
- Although I've been studying Japanese for one year, I couldn't understand what Japanese people said at all when I visited Japan last month.



It's also used to compare someone with someone else. すら or さえ is used with it in this case.

小学生の妹ですらこの算数の問題が解けるというのに、私は解けない。
- Although my little sister, who is an elementary school children, can solve this math problem, I can't.

この漢字はむずかしすぎて日本人でさえ書けない人が多いというのに、そのアメリカ人の少女は全部正確に書いた。
- Despite the fact that these kanji are too difficult and many Japanese people can't write them, the American girl wrote all of them correctly.
