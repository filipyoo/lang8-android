
Using て after an Adjective

て after an adjective is used to list characteristics. で is used when the adjective is a na-adjective.

ラーメンは安くておいしい。
- Ramen is cheap and delicious.

この部屋は広くてたくさんの絵が飾ってある。
- This room is big and has a lot of pictures.
 
彼女は綺麗で賢い。
- She's pretty and smart.


て/で after an adjective is also used to show a reason.

ラーメンは安くて毎日食べられる。
- Ramen is cheap, so you can eat it every day.

それは高くて買えない。
- It's too expensive to buy.

この部屋は広くて、たくさんの絵が飾れる。
- This room is big enough to display a lot of pictures.

この部屋は静かで落ち着く。
- This room is calm, so I feel relaxed.

Since they are used to show reasons, you can rephrase them by using から:

ラーメンは安いから毎日食べられる。
それは高いから買えない。
この部屋は広くからたくさんの絵が飾れる。
この部屋は静かだから落ち着く。


You can't perfectly judge if て after adjectives is used to describe a reason or not by just reading a sentence.

For example,
このポルシェは早くてかっこいい。
This sentence can be read as both "The Porsche is fast and its design looks cool" and "The Porsche is cool because it's fast."
