
Using ～そうだ

When そうだ follows the masu stem of a verb, it means "likely to ～."

雨が振りそうだ。
- It's likely to rain.

彼が選挙に勝ちそうだ。
- He's likely to win the election.

その会社の売上は1000億円に達しそうだ。
- The sales of the company is likely to reach one hundred billion yen.

The opposite is そうにない or そうもない. そうにない has a neutral nuance, and そうもない implies that you're disappointed at the situation or the possibility is less than the sentence that has そうにない.

雨は降りそうにない。
- It's unlikely rain.

彼女は選挙に勝ちそうもない。
- She's more unlikely to win the election. 

彼はいつも達成できそうにない目標を立てる。
- He always sets an unlikely goal to be achieved.
