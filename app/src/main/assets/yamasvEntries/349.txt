
The Usage of 〜ずに(は)いられない

〜ずにいられない and 〜ずにはいられない mean "can't help doing." They follow the a-form of verbs, and they are used to describe your emotion or actions you can't control. The popular song of Elvis Presley "Can't Help Falling In Love With You" is translated as "愛さずにいられない" in Japanese. 


彼のおかしな日記を見た時、笑わずにいられなかった。
I couldn't help laughing when I read his funny journal.

この公園に来ると、子供の頃の楽しかった日々を思い出さずにはいられない。
I always remember my happy days in my childhood whenever I come to this park.

彼らの証言を聞く限り、彼が犯人だと思わずにはいられない。
It's hard not to think that he is the criminal as far as I heard their testimony.

毎日、彼女のことを考えずにはいられない。
It's difficult not to think of her every day.

新しいiPhoneが発売されるたびに、すぐに買わずにいられない。
Every time new iPhone is sold, I can't help buying it at once.
