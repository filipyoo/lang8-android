
Using 〜も〜ずに / 〜も〜ないで

〜も〜ずに is used when you don't do something that is supposed to be done. 

彼はさよならも言わずに帰った。
彼はさよならも言わないで帰った。
He went home without even saying goodbye.

彼は他のメンバーの意見も聞かずにそれを決めた。
彼は他のメンバーの意見も聞かないでそれを決めた。
He decided it without even asking for the other members' opinions.

The も follows a noun as in the sentences above. It can also follow a verb, so you can also say:
彼は他のメンバーの意見を聞きもせずににそれを決めた。
彼は他のメンバーの意見を聞きもしないでそれを決めた。

("意見も聞かずに" sounds as if you are focussing on "意見," and "意見を聞きもせずに" sounds as if you are focussing on the action "聞く." However, there is no major difference between them because 意見を聞く is a set phrase.)





x 彼は砂糖も入れずにコーヒーを飲んだ。
x 彼は箸も使わずに寿司を食べた。
The two sentences above sound unnatural because both drinking coffee without sugar and eating sushi without using chopsticks are normal actions. The first sentence sounds as if you think that to drink black coffee is strange or you know that he always puts sugar in coffee.
In normal cases, you should say:
彼は砂糖「を」入れずにコーヒーを飲んだ - He drank coffee without sugar.
彼は箸「を」使わずに寿司を食べた - He ate sushi without using chopsticks.
