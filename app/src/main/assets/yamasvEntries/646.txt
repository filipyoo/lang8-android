
Using 〜べく

べく can follow the masu stem of a verb to mean "in order to 〜." It's a formal expression.

新しい車を買うべく、貯金をしている。
- I'm saving money to buy a new car.

彼は医者になるべく一生懸命勉強している。
- He is studying hard to become a doctor.

飛行機に間に合うべく急いだ。
- We hastened to the airport to catch our airplane.


When the verb ends with する, すべく sounds more natural.

多くの人がデモに参加すべく集まった。
- A lot of people have gathered to join in a demonstration.

トヨタに対応すべく、日産は新しいハイブリッド車を発表した。
- Nissan has released a new hybrid car against Toyota's one.



〜べくして〜 is used when there is a right reason for the results. The same verb is placed before and after べくして.

君はその試験に受かるべくして受かった
- There is a right reason for the result that you have passed the exam.
This Japanese sentence implies that the speaker knows that the listener had studied hard. On the other hand, 君は試験に落ちるべくして落ちた implies that the speaker knows that the listener didn't study enough to pass it.

彼は大統領に再選されるべくして再選された。
- I knew he re-elected as the president.
This sentence implies that the president has been working hard and a lot of voters acclaim him.
