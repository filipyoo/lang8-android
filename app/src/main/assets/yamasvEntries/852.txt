
Using 人(ひと)

人 has several different meanings. 

1. A person

人が川で泳いでいる。
- A person is swimming in the river.


You can also use 人 to mean "people."

その公園は人でいっぱいだった。
There were a lot of people in the park.


2. Other people

人の意見をもっと注意深く聞くべきだ。
- You should listen to other people's opinion more carefully.

彼は人のアイデアを盗んでその商品を開発した。
- He stole other people's ideas, and developed the product by using the ideas.


3. Human beings

人の脳は他の動物より大きい。
- Brains of human beings are bigger than those of other animals.

When 人 is used to mean "human beings" in scientific articles, it's often written in katakana:
ヒトの脳は他の動物より大きい。


4. Average person

彼の収入は人より多い。
- His income is higher than average.

私の手は人より小さい。
- My hand is smaller than average.
