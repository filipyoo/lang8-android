
Using もっとも

もっとも(最も) means "the most." It's followed by an adjective.

日本で最も高い山は富士山です。
- The highest mountain in Japan is Fuji.

最も美しい花は桜だと思います。
- I think the most beautiful flower is the cherry blossom.

もっとも sounds too formal in a conversation, most people use 一番 instead.


もっとも also can be  used when you agree what someone says. In this case, the kanji is 尤も. Be careful, it's like 犬(dog,) but not the same. It's usually written in hiragana.

あなたの言うことはもっともです。
- I agree with you. / That makes sense.

君の言うことはもっともだが、それをやる時間がない。
- I know you are right, but I don't have time to do it.

怒るのはもっともだが、怒っても問題は解決しない。
- I know you have the right to be angry, but being angry can't solve the problem.


もっとも can be placed at the begin of the sentence to mean "though."

日本の旅行はすばらしかった。もっとも、私がいたのは二日間だけだったけど。
- The trip in Japan was wonderful. I stayed there only two days, though.

彼は私にとって最高の男性です。ハンサムでお金持ちでやさしい。もっとも、彼はもう結婚しているけど。
- He's the perfect guy for me. He's handsome, rich, and kind. He's married, though.
