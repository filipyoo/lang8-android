
How to Say "where am I?" in Japanese

"私はどこ？" sounds very funny. You should say "ここはどこ?" to mean "where am I?"

When you are talking with someone by phone and want to know where the listener is, you can say "そこはどこ? (Where are you now?)"  "今、どこ?" is also often used.

"〜はどこ？" is a popular phrase in casual conversation.

出身はどこ? - Where are you from?
家はどこ? - Where do you live?
お母さんはどこ? - Where is your mother?

"学校はどこ?" can be understood in two different meanings: "Where is your school?" and "Which school do you go to?"
