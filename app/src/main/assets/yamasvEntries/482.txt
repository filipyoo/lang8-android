
The Usage of "〜ないと気がすまない"

"〜ないと気がすまない" is a very common expression. It means "can't settle down until 〜."

彼はいつも言いたいことを全部言わないと気がすまない。
He always can't settle down until he says everything he wants to.

小説を読み始めたら、最後まで読み終わらないと気がすまない。
- Once I start reading a novel,  I can't stop reading until I finish it.

彼に直接会って謝らないと気がすまない。
- I can't settle down until I apologize to him face to face.

彼は、どんな仕事も完璧にしないと気がすまない。
- He isn't satisfied until he finishes any job perfectly.


It's also often used to blame someone.

妹をいじめないと気がすまないの? (いじめるのはやめなさい！)
- Why must you always bully your little sister? (Stop bully her!)

毎晩ビールを飲まないと気がすまないの? 
- Why must you drink beer every single night?
