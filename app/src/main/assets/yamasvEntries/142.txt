
The difference between ばかりです and ところです.

Both ばかりです and ところです is used when you have just completed some actions.

I've just finished writing a report.
ちょうどレポートを書き終わった ところ です。
ちょうどレポートを書き終わった ばかり です。

The both of above sentences have the same meanings, but each sentence is described from a different point of view.

The former sentence is written by focusing on the time, and the latter one is written by focusing on action. ところ refers the time and ばかり refer the action (and the things.)

Therefore, you can say 
ちょうどレポートを書き終わった ところ に、彼が来た
 (He had arrived when I had finished writing a report), but you cannot say 
ちょうどレポートを書き終わった ばかり に、彼が来た.

Similarly, you can say 
これは、私がちょうど書き終わった ばかり のレポートです
 (This is a report which I have just finished writing), but you cannot say 
これは、私がちょうど書き終わった ところ のレポートです.
