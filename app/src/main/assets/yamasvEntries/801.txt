
Using 問題

問題 is a noun that has a lot of meanings.

It can be used to mean "problem."

私の会社には解決すべき問題がたくさんある。
- There are a lot of problems to be solved in my company.

水不足はこの国にとって深刻な問題です。
- Shortage of water is a serious problem for this country.


It also means "trouble."

彼はいつも問題を起こす。
- He always makes trouble.

彼は昨日隣の人と問題を起こした。
- He ran into some trouble with his neighbor yesterday.


It also means "issue."

明日、その問題について話し合う予定です。
- We are going to discuss the issue tomorrow.

大統領はその問題については触れなかった。
- The president didn't refer to the issue.


It also means "question."

この数学の問題はとてもむずかしい。
- This math question is very difficult.

However, you should use "質問" when you ask a question. "問題があります" sounds as if you are saying "I have a problem." You should say "質問があります" to mean "I have a question." "質問" is used when the speaker is supposed to be able to answer with ease. If you give someone a math problem, you should say not "問題をあげます" but "問題を出します."


問題 is also used to mean "point."

そういう問題じゃない。
- That's not the point.

それはまた別な問題です。
- That's beside the point.


”問題の" is used to modify a person or thing everybody talks about. It's the same as "噂の."

彼が問題の人です。
彼が噂の人です。
- He is the man everybody talks about.

"問題の" has negative nuance a little; "噂の" doesn't.

x 彼が、ノーベル賞を取ると言われている問題の科学者です。
o 彼が、ノーベル賞を取ると言われている噂の科学者です。
- He is the scientist everybody talks about, who is expected to win the Nobel Prize.

o 彼が、データを捏造したと疑われている問題の科学者です。
o 彼が、データを捏造したと疑われている噂の科学者です。
- He is the scientist everybody talks about, who is suspected to fabricate the data.
