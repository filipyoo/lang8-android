
Using 〜に限って

"〜時に限って" is used when something always happens in some conditions. The following Japanese sentences are a little exaggerated, but often used.

傘を忘れた時に限って雨が降る。
- It rains whenever I forget to bring my umbrella.

必要な時に限って上司はいない。
- Out boss would be absent when we need him.

急いでいる時に限って、その通りは混んでいる。
- The street is always busy when I'm in a hurry.


"〜に限って〜ない" is used to describe that something never happens.

彼に限って嘘はつかない。
- He never lies.

彼女に限ってそんな失礼なことは言わない。
- She is the last person to say such rude things.


〜に限って is also used when people or organizations in some group have the other same characteristics. It's often used in paradoxical sentences.

金持ちに限って無駄遣いをしない。
- Many of rich people doesn't waste their money.

無能な上司に限って威張っている。
- An incompetent boss tends to act big.

「日本語は簡単だ」と言っている外国人に限って、日本語がうまくない。
- Most of the foreign people who say that Japanese is easy are not good at Japanese.
