
Using 案の定(あんのじょう)

案の定 is used when what you expected happens. 


案の定、彼は現れなかった。
I expected him to not appear and it was true.

案の定、彼女は立候補した。
I thought she would stand as a candidate, and sure enough, she did.

案の定、彼は私の言ったことに反対した。
As I had thought, he disagreed with what I said.


You can't use it for the future.

案の定、彼は私の言うことに反対するだろう。(This sentence is incorrect.)
He will surely disagree with what I say.
