
How to translate "while" into Japanese

"While" is a difficult conjunction for some Japanese people because it can be translated into Japanese in  many ways by the context. On the other hand, I sometimes read an unnatural Japanese translation of "while" by English speakers.

1. ながら
When you use "while" to describe that you are doing two actions at the same time, ながら sounds natural.

私はいつも朝食をとりながら新聞を読みます。
- I always read the newspaper while eating breakfast.

彼女は看護婦として働きながら、夜間大学で勉強している。
- She studies at a night college while working as a nurse.

You can't say ながら when the subjects of the two actions are different. For example, "私は朝食をとりながら、父は新聞を読みます" is incorrect.

2. 〜間
When the action is being done all the time that something is happening, you should say "〜間."

私が朝食を食べている間、彼はいつも新聞を読んでいる。
- He always reads the newspaper while I am eating breakfast.

私がシャワーを浴びる間、ここで待っていてくれる？
- Can you wait here while I take a shower?

3. 〜時
When the action is done during the time that something is happening, ”〜時" sounds natural.

そのカフェでコーヒーを飲んでいる時、彼を見た。
- I saw him while drinking coffee at the cafe.

弟が出かけている時、弟のPCを壊してしまった。
- I broke my brother's PC while he was gone.

4. 一方
When "while" is used to show a contrast, you can say 一方.

彼はとても金持ちだが、一方彼の弟は貧しい。
- He is very rich, while his brother is poor.

彼女は数学はとても得意だが、その一方、運動は苦手だ。
- She is good at math, while she isn't good at sports.

5. ながら to mean "although."
Interestingly, like "while" in English, ながら can be also used to mean "although."

彼は英語の先生でありながら、時々文法を間違っている。
- While he is an English teacher, he sometimes makes grammatical mistakes.

彼女はまだ５歳ながら、たくさん漢字を知っている。
- While she is only five-years-old, she knows a lot of kanji.
