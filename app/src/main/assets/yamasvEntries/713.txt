
Present Perfect Continuous In Japanese

When you have been doing something, but you don't do it now, you can say ていた. In a casual conversation, い would be omitted.

何をしていたの？ずっと探していたんだよ。
何してたの？ずっと探してたんだよ。
- What have you been doing? I've been looking for you.

あの本屋でマンガを読んでいた。
あの本屋でマンガを読んでた。
- I've been reading manga at that bookstore.


If you are continuing it, you can say ている.

私は彼を30分間捜している。
- I've been looking for him for a half hours.

彼はマンガを一日中読んでいる。
- He has been reading a manga all the day.


If you have been doing something for very long time, you can say てきた.

私はトヨタで30年間働いてきた。
- I've been working for Toyota for 30 years.

私は子供の時からヤンキースの試合を見てきた。
- I've been watching the Yankees' game since I was a boy.

てきた doesn't imply whether you are doing it now or not.

私はトヨタで30年間働いてきたが、先週辞めた。
- I've been working for Toyota for 30 years, but I quit last week.

私は子供の時からヤンキースの試合を見てきた。これからも死ぬまで見続ける。
- I've been watching the Yankees' game since I was a boy, and I'm going to be watching until I die.
