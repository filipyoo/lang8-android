
Using 気になる

気になる is used whey you wonder something.

明日雨が降るか気になる。
- I wonder if it's raining tomorrow or not.


It's also used whey you are worried about something.

仕事の面接の結果が気になって会話に集中できない。
- Worried about the result of the job interview, I can't concentrate on conversations.

増税の影響が気になっている。景気後退を招きかねない。
- I'm worrying about the effects of the tax increase. It could bring a recession.


It's also used whey you are interested in or curious about something. You can use it for both things or people.

新しい上司が気になる。ハンサムで頭がいい。独身だったらいいのに。
- I'm interested in my new boss. He's handsome and smart. I hope he's a single.

新しいアウディのA4が気になっている。一回試乗してみたい。
- I'm interested in the new Audi A4. I'd like to test drive it once.


You can use it to refer to a disadvantage or something that makes you uncomfortable.

彼はいい選手だけど、気が短いのが気になる。
- He's a good player, but his disadvantage is that he's a little impatient.

この車のシートが硬すぎるのが気になる。
- The seats of this car is too hard. It makes me uncomfortable.
