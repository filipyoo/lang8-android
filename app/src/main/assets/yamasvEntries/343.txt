
The Usage of 貸す/借りる

貸す means "lend / lease,"  and 借りる means "borrow / rent." Unlike English, you can use 貸す/借りる whether you pay for it or not.

傘を借りていい？ - Can I borrow your umbrella? 
傘を貸してくれる? - Can you lend me your umbrella?

この部屋を一年間借りたい - I'd like to rent this room for one year.
この部屋を一年間貸してくれますか? - Can you rent out this room to me for one year?


貸す and 借りる can be used for bathroom (toilet) or telephone.
トイレを借りていいですか?　- Can I use your bathroom? 
電話を貸してください - Please let me use your phone. 
These are more common expressions than "使っていいですか/使わしてください."


"手を貸す/借りる" are idioms to mean "to help / to give a hand."
手を貸して - Please give me your hand.
手を借りたい - I want you to help me.

Sometimes, "貸す/借りる" is used for a person. "彼を借りたい" means "We want him to help us."


借り and 貸し are nouns.
I owe you. - 私はあなたに借りがある = あなたは私に貸しがある
You owe me. - あなたは私に借りがある = 私はあなたに貸しがある
