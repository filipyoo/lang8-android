
Using まで

金曜日までここにいます。
- I'll stay here until Friday.

1から10まで日本語で言える？
- Can you say 1 to 10 in Japanese?

コンサートまで後30分です。
- It's thirty minutes before the concert.

母親まで私のことを嫌っている。
- Even my mother hates me.

どこ行くの？
渋谷まで。
- Where are you going?
- Shibuya.

The last usage may sound unnatural to you, but it's a very natural way to describe where you go, especially when you tell a taxi driver your destination.

東京駅まで行ってください。/ 東京駅までお願いします。
- Could you take me to Tokyo station?
("東京駅に行ってください" is also correct, but most native speakers would use まで.)

このバスは東京駅まで行きますか?
- Does this bus go to Tokyo station?
(This Japanese sentence doesn't mean that the speaker asks if the last stop of the bus is Tokyo station.)

日本までマンガを買いに行く予定だ。
- I'm going to Japan to buy manga.

駅までの道順を教えて下さい。
あの信号まで真っすぐ行って、右に曲がってください。
- Can you tell me how to get to the station?
- Go straight and turn right at that traffic light.
