
Using 〜はいいが

The past form of a verb + はいいが is used when what you did doesn't bring a good result. (The は is pronounced as "wa.")

エレガントなドレスを買ったはいいが、着る機会がない。
- I bought an elegant dress, but I have no opportunity to wear it.

大学を出たはいいが、仕事が見つからない。
- I graduated from university, but haven't found a job.

日本でたくさんマンガを買ったはいいが、日本語がよくわからない。マンガの中の日本語は教科書と全く違う。
- I bought a lot of manga in Japan, but I don't understand Japanese in it well. It's completely different from what appears in my textbook.

日本で働くことになったはいいが、同僚はみな英語を話すので、僕の日本語は全く上達していない。
- I moved to Japan to work, but all my colleagues speak English, so my Japanese hasn't improved at all.
