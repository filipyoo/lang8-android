
How to clear what you want to negate.

彼女とこの店でお酒を飲みたくない。

This sentence is confusing. It is possible to understand that "彼女と飲みたくない", "この店で飲みたくない", and "お酒を飲みたくない."

You can use は to clear what you want to negate.

(彼女が嫌いなので、) 彼女と は この店でお酒を飲みたくない。  
(I don't want to spend time with her.)

(私はお酒を飲むとすぐねむくなるので、) 彼女とこの店で は お酒を飲みたくない。  
(I don't want to drink alcohol in this restaurant because I fall asleep as soon as I drink alcohol. It's ok to drink in my house.)

(私はお酒が嫌いなので、)彼女とこの店でお酒 は 飲みたくない。  
(I don't want to drink alcohol because I hate it.)



誰も彼に賛成しませんでした。
None of them agreed with him.

誰も は 彼に賛成しませんでした。(This sentence implies that most of them agreed with him.)
Not all of them agreed with him.


彼女 は 彼に賛成しませんでした。
If you put stress on は in this sentence, the listeners can easily understand that most people agreed with him. (She might be the only one who disagrees with him.)

日本旅行中、京都に は 行きませんでした。
Similarly, if you put stress on は in this sentence, the listeners can understand that you had visited many other places in Japan.
