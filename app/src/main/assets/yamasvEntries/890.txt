
Using 買う

買う means to buy, but it's sometimes used for something you can't buy with money.

買う can be used to mean to incur someone's negative emotions, such as wrath, antipathy, etc.

役人たちの不正は世論の怒りを買った。
- The unfairness of public officers incurred the wrath of public opinion.

彼の失礼な態度は客の反感を買った。
- His rude attitudes incurred client's antipathy.

最近、多くの観光客が道にゴミを捨てて、地元の人のひんしゅくを買っている。
- Lately, a lot of tourists throw garbage on street and it makes local people disgusted.


失笑を買う means to be laughed at.

その記者は大統領に的はずれな質問をして、失笑を買った。
- The journalist asked a irrelevant question to the president and was laughed at by everybody.


ケンカを買う means to take up a quarrel. 売られたケンカは買う( "I will do it if I am dared to.") is a common phrase.


買って出る means to volunteer. 

友達が引っ越しの手伝いを買って出てくれた。
- My friends volunteered to help with our moving.

夫はいつもリトルリーグのアンパイアを買って出る。彼は本当に野球と子供が好きだ。
- My husband always volunteers Little League umpire. He really likes baseball and kids.
