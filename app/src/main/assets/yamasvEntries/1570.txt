
Using そこそこ

そこそこ is used when something is slightly better than the average. It comes before the word it modifies.

この料理はそこそこ美味しい。
- This dish isn't bad.

彼はそこそこ稼いでいるが、あまり幸せそうじゃない。
- He's making a not-so-bad income, but he doesn't seem to be happy.


そこそこ can also follow a number when the real number is not much larger than the number. It's often used when the number is too small than expected.

日本ではラーメンはそんなに高くない。6ドルそこそこで食べられる。
- Ramen isn't so expensive in Japan. You can have it for only 6 dollars or so.

彼は6ヶ月そこそこで日本語をマスターした。
- He mastered Japanese for only about 6 months.

彼は20才そこそこで２つの博士号をとった。
- He got two Ph.Ds when he was only 20 years old or so.
