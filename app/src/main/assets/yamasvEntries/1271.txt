
How to Say "can afford" in Japanese

You can say 余裕がある to mean "can afford." 

ポルシェを買う余裕はない。
- I can't afford Porsche.
You can also say ポルシェは買えない, but it's a little ambiguous. The listener might think that it's because it's out of stock. Of course, you can say it if it's clear from the context.

ポルシェを買う余裕があったら、とっくに買っている。
- If I could afford Porsche, I would've already purchesed it. 

忙しくて息子の世話をする余裕が無い。
- I'm busy. I cannot afford to take care of my son.

余裕があるならこの最新のPCを買ったほうがいい。
- You should get this cutting-edge PC if you can afford it. 


When you are talking about the future, you need to say 余裕ができる.

余裕ができたら絶対に買う。
- I'll definitely buy it when I can afford it.


許されない sounds natural when you say "cannot afford" in a serious condition.

前半だけでもう2点取られた。もう失点は許されない。
- We conceded two goals already in the first half. We can't afford to concede more goals.

このテストは弁護士になる最後のチャンスだ。ミスは許されない。
- This test is the last chance for me to become a lawyer. I can't afford to make mistakes.
