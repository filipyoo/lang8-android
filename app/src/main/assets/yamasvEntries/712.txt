
Using ているところ and たところ

ているところ is used when you are doing something.

何をしているの? - What are you doing?

日本語を勉強しているところ。- I'm studying Japanese.
昼食を食べているところ。- I'm having lunch.

When you ask "What are you doing?" by phone, "何をしていたの?" sounds more natural because the listener must have stopped some action to answer the phone. You can answer "していたところ" even if you haven't finished the action.

何をしていたの? - What are you doing?
昼食を食べていたところ。- I'm having lunch.
(This answer doesn't mean that you have finished the lunch.)


たところ is used when you have just finished some action.

家についたところです。
- I've just arrived home.

昼食を食べ終わったところに彼が訪ねてきた。
- He visited me when I had just finished lunch.
