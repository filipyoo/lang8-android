
Using 価値 and 甲斐

Both 価値(かち) and 甲斐(かい) mean "value." Both can be followed by "がある," but they are not interchangeable. 甲斐 is mainly used when you are talking about past events. 価値 isn't.

京都はとても綺麗なところだ。行く価値がある。
- Kyoto is a very beautiful place. It's worth to visit.

京都はとても綺麗なところだった。行った甲斐があった。
- Kyoto was a very beautiful place. It was worth to visit.

この本はとてもおもしろい。読む価値があるよ。
- This book is very interesting. You should read it.

この本はとてもおもしろかった。高い金を払った甲斐があった。
- This book was very interesting. It was worth the expensive price.


甲斐 can follow the masu-stem of verbs. It's pronounced as がい not かい. In this case, you can use it in present tense too.

あなたにとって働き甲斐とはなんですか？
- How do you define job satisfaction.

この仕事が私の生き甲斐です。
- This job is my reason for living.

彼は飲み込みが早くて教え甲斐がある。
- He's a fast learner. It's a pleasure to teach him.
(飲み込みが早い means to be a fast learner.)
