
Idioms Using 気


・気が知れない is used when you can't understand someone.

真冬に泳ぐなんて彼の気が知れない。
I can't understand him, who is swimming in a river in the middle of winter.


・気が向かない is used when you don't want doing something or hesitate to do something. 気が進まない is the same as 気が向かない. 
気が向く is used when you feel like doing something. 気が進む isn't often used.

こんな寒い日に買い物に出かけるのは気が向かない。
こんな寒い日に買い物に出かけるのは気が進まない。
I don't want to go shopping on such a cold day.

気が向いたら、後で買い物に行く。
I'll go shopping later if I'm inclined to.


・気が重い is similar to 気が向かない, but it's used in slightly more serious cases. It's used when you don't want to do something, but you have to.

彼らに悪いニュースを伝えないといけないので、今日は気が重い。
I'm feeling down today because I have to tell them the bad news.


・気が短い is used to describe a person who is quick-tempered. 

彼は気が短い。
He is quick-tempered.

この仕事は気が短い人には向かない。
This job is not suitable for a person who is quick-tempered.


・気が早い is used when you are preparing something too early. For example, you are thinking baby names even though you are not pregnant, or you are preparing to move after you had a job interview even though they haven't decided to hire you yet.



You can also use "気の〜" instead of "気が〜" when it modifies a noun. When it's used as a predicate, you can't use it.

○ 彼は気が短い。
x 彼は気の短い。
○この仕事は気が短い人には向かない。
○この仕事は気の短い人には向かない。

○ 彼らに悪いニュースを伝えないといけないので、今日は気が重い。
x 彼らに悪いニュースを伝えないといけないので、今日は気の重い。
○ 悪いニュースを伝えるのは、気が重い仕事だ。
○ 悪いニュースを伝えるのは、気の重い仕事だ。
- It's a depressing job to tell someone bad news.
