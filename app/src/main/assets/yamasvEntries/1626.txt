
Using たかが

たかが is used to stress that something is insignificant. 

たかがデートに履いていく靴を選ぶのに30分かかった。
- I took 30 minutes just to choose shoes I wore for the date.

これを観るまでたかがアニメと思っていた。
- I had thought that the anime was insignificant until I watched this.

落ち着いて。裁判じゃなくてたかが面接だから。
- Take it easy. It's just a job interview, not a trial.


たかだか is used to stress that a number that follows it is small.

日本じゃ一番有名なラーメン屋に行ってもたかだか10ドルで最高のラーメンが食べられる。
- You can have the best ramen for only 10 dollars in Japan even if you go to the most famous restaurant.

タクシーを呼ぶ必要はありません。そこまで歩いてたかだか10分です。
- You don't have to call a taxi. It'll take only ten minutes to go there on foot.

たかだか30ドル稼ぐのに10時間働かないといけない。
- I have to work for 10 hours only to earn 30 dollars.
