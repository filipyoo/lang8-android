
Using この/ここ〜

When この or ここ is followed by a period or time, it means "in the last 〜."

この/ここ 3日間何も食べていない。
- I haven't eaten anything in the last two days.

この/ここ 3年間毎日日本語を勉強してきた。
- I have studied Japanese everyday in the last three years.

この/ここ 数年テレビを見ていない。
- I haven't seen TV in the last few years.


When you want to say "for the next 〜," you can say "この先."

この先2年間日本で働く予定です。
- I'm going to work in Japan for the next two years.

この先数ヶ月は会えないだろう。
- We won't see for the next few months.

この先数日は雨が降り続くでしょう。
- It will keep raining for the next few days.
