
The Usage of わけない

When わけない comes after the potential form, it means "impossible." This is a short form of わけがない. It's mainly used in spoken Japanese.

こんなむずかしい問題が解ける わけない.
It's impossible for me to solve such a difficult problem.

私が英語を流暢に話せる わけない.
It's impossible for me to speak English fluently.


わけない is also used as an adjectives to mean "very easy."

この問題を解くのは わけない.
It's very easy for me to solve this problem.

彼をだますのは わけない.
It's very easy to deceive him.

こんなの わけない よ.
It's a piece of cake.
