
Using じゃない

じゃない is a casual version of "ではない," which is used to negate something. It can follow a noun or the stem of na-adjectives. 

私は日本人じゃない。
- I'm not Japanese. 

この花は綺麗じゃない。
- This flower isn't beautiful.

(Beginners sometimes use it after an I-adjectives, like "美しいじゃない/楽しいじゃない",  but it's incorrect. You need to say "美しくない/楽しくない.")


じゃない is also used to stress a phrase which is followed by it, or to show your surprise. In this case, it can follow a noun, a na-adjective, and a verb.

レイチェルじゃない!　久しぶり!
- Hey, Rachel! Long time no see!
(This Japanese sentence implies that you run into Rachel.)

この花、すごく綺麗じゃない！
この花、すごく美しいじゃない！
- This flower is very beautiful, isn't it!?
(In this case, you can use it after an i-adjectives.)

それに触るなって言ったじゃない！　なんで言うことを聞かないの？
- I told you not to touch it! Why you don't listen to me?

タバコは止めるって約束したじゃない！
- You promised me to quit smoking! 

The formal version of this じゃない is じゃないですか:
レイチェルじゃないですか!　お久しぶりです!
この花はすごく綺麗じゃないですか！
それに触るなって言ったじゃないですか！　なんで言うことを聞かないんですか？
タバコは止めるって約束したじゃないですか！



You can't judge whether a じゃない is used to negate something or to stress something by just reading a sentence. You need to judge from the context, their tone of voice, or their expression.
