
How to Say "Why not?" Casually in Japanese

When you ask the reason someone suggests you not to do something, you can say "なんでだめなの?”

その車は買わないほうがいいよ。
なんでだめなの？ 
- You shouldn't buy the car.
- Why not?

You can simply say "なんで?" in this case. It's a convenient phrase because it also can be used when someone suggests you to do something.

その車は買わないほうがいいよ。
なんで？ 

その車を買ったほうがいいよ。
なんで？ 
- You should buy the car.
- Why?



When you accept someone's suggestion, you can say "そうだね" or "もちろん."

ピザやハンバーガーの代わりに日本料理を食べたほうがいいと思う。
そうだね。おいしいしヘルシーだし。
- I think we should eat Japanese food instead of pizza or hamburgers.
- Yeah, why not? It's good and healthy.

ワインをもう一杯いかがですか？
もちろん。
- Would you like another glass of wine?
- Sure, why not?

 

When you suggest someone to do something, you can say "たら?"

PCが遅すぎる。
新しいの買ったら？　もっといいやつが手頃な値段で売ってるよ。
- My PC is very slow.
- Why not buy new one? They are selling higher spec ones for reasonable prices.

東京に行きたいけど飛行機代が高い。
バスで行ったら？時間はかかるけど安いよ。
- I'd like to go to Tokyo, but the plane ticket is expensive.
- Why not take a bus? It takes more time, but is cheap.
