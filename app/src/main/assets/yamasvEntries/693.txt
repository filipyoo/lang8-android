
Using たまたま and 偶然

たまたま and 偶然 mean "by chance." They can modify a verb.

駅で 偶然/たまたま 高校の先生に会った。
- I happened to see my high school teacher at the station.

本屋で 偶然/たまたま この本を見つけた。
- I came across this book in a bookstore.

You can also say 偶然に, but I think 偶然 sounds more natural in the sentence above.


You can also use them as the predicate.

勝ったのは 偶然/たまたま です。私が彼より上手かったわけじゃない。
- I won the game by chance. It's not because I was better than him.

君と同じ車を勝ったのは 偶然/たまたま だ。同じ車を持ってるとは知らなかった。
- I happened to buy the same car as yours. I didn't know that you own the same one.


You can start a sentence with 偶然にも, but you can't say たまたまにも.

偶然にも、妻と私は誕生日が同じです。
- As it happens, my wife and I have the same birthday.
