
The Usage of 〜わけにはいかない

〜わけにはいかない means "cannot do something." It's not used when you can't do it physically or your ability isn't enough to do it. It's used when you can't do it under the situation or as your policy.

我々のプロジェクトは遅れてるから、今、長い休みをとるわけにはいかない。
Our project is behind the schedule, so I can't take a long vacation now.

金が必要だけど、その金を借りるわけにはいかない。それは君の大学のための金だ。
I need money, but I can't borrow this money. This is for your university.

親友に嘘をつくわけにはいかない。
I can't lie to my best friend.

客を長く待たせるわけにはいかないから、もう行くよ。
I'll go because i can't let my client wait too long.

優勝するためには、次の試合に負けるわけにはいかない。
We can't lose the next game in order to win the championship.

君は10代だからビールを飲ませるわけにはいかない。
I can't serve you beer because you are a teenager.



? 私はボルトより早く走るわけにはいかない
This sentence implies that the speaker can run faster than Bolt if necessary but the speaker won't run faster than he intentionally. You should say "私はボルトより早く走れない" to mean "I can't run faster than Bolt."

? 日本語を流暢に話すわけにはいかない
Similarly, this sentence implies that the speaker can speak Japanese fluently if neccessary but they don't speak it fluently because of some reasons.
