
Using だっけ and たっけ

だっけ is used when you ask something you forgot. When it follows a verb, ん follows the verb.

彼の名前は何だっけ？
彼の名前は何て言うんだっけ?
- What's his name, again? 

日本に行くのはいつだっけ？
いつ日本に行くんだっけ？
- When will you go to Japan, again?

日本語で「thank you」は何だっけ?
日本語で「thank you」は何と言うんだっけ?
- What do you say "thank you" in Japanese, again?

日本に行くにはビザが要るんだっけ？
- Do I need visa for Japan, again?



たっけ is used when you are not sure whether you did something or not, or forgot when/how/where did you do something.

ドアに鍵を掛けたっけ？
- Did I lock the door?

前に会ったっけ?
- Have I met you before? / Do I know you?

どこで会ったっけ?
- Where did we meet?

いつ日本に行ったっけ?
- When did I go to Japan?
