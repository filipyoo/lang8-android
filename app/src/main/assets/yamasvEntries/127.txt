
にしても

1.
にしても means "even if." It will follow a subjective sentence and be followed by a speaker's opinion or the fact:

貧しい家に生まれたにしても、成功することは可能です。
Even if you come from a poor family, it is possible to succeed.

喉が渇いていないにしても、夏は水分を摂ることが大事です。
It's important to drink fluids in summer even if not thirsty.

試験に落ちたにしても、恥じることはありません。
Even if you would fail the exam, you would not have to be ashamed it.

2.
You can also show your some understanding by using it. It will follow a your understanding and be followed by a speaker's opinion or the fact:

彼が嘘をついたにしても、暴力はいけない。
Even if he lied, it can be no excuse for violence.

渋滞しているにしても、彼は遅すぎる。
I know traffic is slow but he is too late.

Sometimes it will follow a noun in this case:

冗談にしても、やり過ぎだ。
Even in a joke, you've crossed the line.


3.
You can also use it as a means of "even for."

日本人にしても、一部の漢字はむずかしい。
Even for Japanese, some kanji are difficult.
