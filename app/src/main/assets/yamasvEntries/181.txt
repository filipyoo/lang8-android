
A very weird Japanese expression "ではないか."

When ではない comes after some clause, it negates that clause. However, ではないか is NOT used to negate; it is used to express your guess or thought. Japanese people often use ではないか.
ではない is mainly used in written language. じゃない is used in conversation.


彼は日本人 ではない と思う。
- I don't think he is Japanese.

彼は日本人 ではないか と思う。
- I think/guess he is Japanese.


彼が食べたの ではない と思う。
- I don't think he ate it.

彼が食べたの ではないか と思う。
- I think/guess he ate it.


これは、私の車じゃない。
- This is not my car.

これは、私の車じゃないか!
- This is my car!
This expression is used when the speaker is surprised to find out something.


じゃないか is also used to invite.

飲みに行こうじゃないか。
- Let's go for a drink.

ゲームで遊ぼうじゃないか。
- Let's play video games.


じゃない is sometimes used when the speaker is surprised.

田中さんじゃない！ 久しぶり。
- Hey Tanaka-san, it's been such a long time.

Since じゃない is used to negate a clause sentence or to express surprise, you have to judge what it means from the context when it is used in conversation.
