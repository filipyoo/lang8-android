
Using 〜らしい

らしい is used to tell hearsay.

新しい社長は日本人らしい。
- They say that the new president is Japanese.

その試合は中止になったらしい。
- I heard that the match was canceled.


It's also used when someone's behavior is typical of some categories of people. It's often used with いかにも.

彼は男らしい男だ。
- He's a manly man.

その考え方はいかにも日本人らしい。
- The way of thinking is typical of Japanese people. 

彼はいかにも銀行家らしいきちんと仕立てられたスーツをいつも着ている。
- He always wears a neat tailored suit; it's typical of a banker.

You can also use it for things.

これはいかにも日本らしいおみやげだ。
- This is a very Japanese souvenir.

このスムーズな乗り心地はいかにもメルセデスらしい。
- This smooth ride is quintessentially Mercedes.


You can't say メルセデスらしい when you are talking about another maker's car. You need to say "まるで〜のようだ." 

このトヨタのスムーズな乗り心地はまるでメルセデスのようだ。
This Toyota's smooth ride reminds me a Mercedes.

彼はまるで銀行家のようなきちんと仕立てられたスーツをいつも着ている。
- He always wears a neat tailored suit, as if he's a banker.
(This sentence implies that he's not a banker.)
