
How to Make Japanese Verbs Honorific

Keigo is very difficult for Japanese learners, but there are simple ways to make verbs honorific. You can use the passive form of verbs as the honorific form in many cases.
For example, 行かれる is the passive form of 行く, to go, and it's also used as the honorific form of it. When someone uses the passive form of a verb, but you don't understand why the passive form is used, it could be the honorific form.

どこに 行かれる のですか?
- Where are you going to?

この本はもう 読まれ ましたか?
- Have you read this book yet?

あなたがこの本を 書かれた とは知りませんでした。
- I didn't know that you wrote this book.

田中さんはもう家に 帰られ ました。
- Mr Tanaka has already gone home.



お + the masu-stem of a verb + になる is also used as the honorific form of the verb.

どこに お行きになる のですか?
この本はもう お読みになり ましたか?
あなたがこの本を お書きになった とは知りませんでした。
田中さんはもう家に お帰りになり ました。
