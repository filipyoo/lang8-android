
Using とりわけ

とりわけ means "in particular" or "among others." It's used when you pick out something from what you mentioned before.

日本の料理はみんな美味しかった。とりわけ、寿司はすごく美味しかった。
- All the Japanese food was good. Sushi in particular was delicious.

みんなその泥棒に怒っている。とりわけ、高い指輪を盗まれたジュンはカンカンだ。
- Everybody is angry with the thief. Especially, Jun, who had her expensive ring stolen, is hitting the ceiling.

日本の色んな所を訪れたけど、とりわけ京都は印象深かった。
- I've visited a lot of areas in Japan. Among all, Kyoto was very impressive.

You can say 中でも instead.



It's also used as an adverb to mean "especially/particularly."

今夜はとりわけ寒い。
- It's especially cold tonight.

この薬はとりわけインフルエンザに効く。
- This medicine works particularly well for the flu.

このアニメはとりわけ小さい男の子に人気がある。
- This anime is particularly popular with little boys.
