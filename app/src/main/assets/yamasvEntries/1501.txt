
Using 〜てもいいくらいだ

〜もいいくらいだ follows the te-form of a verb to mean "I wouldn't mind doing the action." It's used when you are very impressed by something and it follows a slightly exaggerated example.

映画はどうだった？
明日もう一回見てもいいくらいだ。
- How did you like the movie?
- I wouldn't mind seeing it tomorrow again.

昨日泊まったホテルはすごく快適だった。ずっと住んでもいいくらいだ。
- The hotel room I stayed yesterday was very comfortable. I wouldn't mind living there for good.

日本のラーメンは本当に美味しい。毎日食べてもいいくらいです。
- The ramen in Japan is really good. I wouldn't mind having it every day.

彼の下で働くのはすごく勉強になる。ただで働いてもいいくらいだ。
- I can learn a lot from working for him. I wouldn't mind working for free.


If you merely say 〜てもいい, it means "I/You can do something if you want."

駅にはどう行けばいいですか？
そこまで連れて行ってもいいですよ。
- How can I get to the station?
- I can take you there if you want.

暑かったら窓を開けてもいいですよ。
- I don't mind opening the window if it's hot.

車が必要だったら私のを使ってもいいよ。
- You can use my car if you need.
