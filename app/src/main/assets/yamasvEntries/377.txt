
How to Say "Should Have ...." Casually in Japanese

"Should have ..." is translated as "〜べきだった" in many textbooks. However, it sounds too formal in casual conversations. You can say "〜ばよかった" or "〜たらよかった" in casual conversations.

When the subject is not yourself, you can say both "〜ばよかったのに" and "〜たらよかったのに."

Their concert was really fantastic! You should've come with us.
彼らのコンサートはすごく良かった。一緒に来ればよかったのに/一緒に来たらよかったのに。

You should've visited Japan in Spring if you wanted to see cherry blossoms.
桜が見たかったのなら、春に日本に行けばよかったのに/春に行ったら良かったのに。


When the subject is yourself, you can't say "〜のに." Furthermore, "〜たらよかった" sounds a little bit unnatural for me.

I heard that their concert was really fantastic. I should've gone with you.
彼らのコンサートはすごく良かったらしい。一緒に行けばよかった。

I should've studied French harder when I was in high school. It's difficult to learn foreign languages after I get older.
高校の時にもっと一生懸命フランス語を勉強しておけばよかった。年をとってから外国語を勉強するのはむずかしい。

I should've realized much earlier that she was a wonderful woman.
彼女が素晴らしい女性だってことにもっと早く気付けばよかった。
