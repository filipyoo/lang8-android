
I-form of verbs + 合う

When 合う follows the I-form of some verbs, it means "to do each other."

あなたは日本人で、私はアメリカ人だから、お互いに言語を教え合えます。
We can teach our native language each other because you're Japanese and I'm an American.

彼らはいつも悪口を言い合っている。
They are always saying something snide each other.

あの兄弟は、互いに助けあって生きてきた。
That brother has lived by helping each other.

我々は、長い間その問題について話し合ってきた。
We have discussed this matter for a long time.


"彼を知ってますか?" doesn't mean "Do you know him personally?" That means "Do you know who he is?" Therefore, when someone asks you "ブラッド・ピットを知ってますか," you can say "知ってます" if you know that Brad Pitt is a famous actor. If you want to say "Do you know Brad Pitt personally?" you should say "ブラッド・ピットと知り合いですか?"
