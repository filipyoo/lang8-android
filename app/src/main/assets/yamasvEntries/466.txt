
なる and する

If your company transfers you to London, you can't say "ロンドンに転勤することにしました." Since "しました" is used only when you made the decision, you have to say "転勤することになりました" in this case.

However, the opposite isn't always true. "なりました" is sometimes used when you made the decision.  You can say both "結婚することにしました" and "結婚することになりました" to say "We are getting married." (Of course, you and your partner had made the decision in this case.)

I have decided to study in Japan. / I'm going to study in Japan.
You should say "日本に留学することにしました" at the time you have decided, but you can say "日本に留学することにしました/なりました" after a few days from the decision.
"することにしました" implies that you could decide it by only your judge. If it's needed to pass an exam and you've passed the exam, you should say "できることになりました."

I can study in Japan because I've passed the exam.
試験に合格したから、日本で勉強できることになりました.
(In this case, you can't say "できることにしました.")

I can go to Japan because my uncle gave me the plane ticket.
おじさんが飛行機のチケットをくれたから、日本に行けることになった。
("日本に行けることになった" implies that you are eager to visit japan. "日本に行くことにした" sounds as if you are not interested in Japan so much.)
