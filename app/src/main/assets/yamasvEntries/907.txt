
Using だけの to Mean "enough"

だけの can be used to mean "enough." It follows the potential form of verbs and is followed by a noun. (The dictionary form is sometimes used, but the potential form is mainly used.)

彼はエンパイアステートビルを買えるだけのお金を持っている。
- He has money enough to buy the empire state building.

自転車を買えるだけの金さえ持ってない。
- I don't have enough money even to buy a bike.

彼女にはオリンピック選手になれるだけの才能がある。
- She has a talent enough to become an Olympic athlete. 

生活出来るだけの収入があれば幸せです。
- I'm happy if I have income enough to live.

卒業までに、新聞が読めるだけの日本語の語彙を覚えたい。
- I'd like to get Japanese vocabulary enough to read a newspaper by graduation.
