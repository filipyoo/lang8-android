
Using 出る

出る means "go/come out." 出てくる sounds more natural when you saw someone coming out from somewhere.

試合が終わった後、たくさんの人がスタジアムから出てきた。
- A lot of people came out from the stadium after the game was finished.


出てきた can be used when you found something by accident.

部屋を整理していた時、子供の頃撮った古い写真が出てきた。
- Organizing my room, I found an old picture I took when I was a child.

ジーンズのポケットから見慣れない鍵が出てきた。
- I found an unfamiliar key in my jeans pocket.


出る can be used to mean to leave.

私は毎朝7時に家を出る。
- I leave home at seven every morning.
(家を出る is also used when you leave home and live somewhere else.)

彼は1時間前に会社を出たから、もうすぐそこに着くだろう。
- He left our office one hour ago, so he should arrive there soon.


It's also used to mean to answer a phone or the door.

昨夜何度も電話したのに彼は全然出なかった。
- I phoned him many times last night, but he didn't answer at all.

誰かが来ると、3歳の娘がいつも出る。
- My daughter, who is three years old, always answers the door when someone comes.


It's also used when you appear on TV or in a movie.

今夜テレビ番組に出る予定だ。
- I'm going to take part in a TV show tonight.

彼はアクション映画によく出る役者だ。
- He's an actor who often appears in action movies.


It's also used to mean to graduate from a school. It sounds more natural than 卒業する in a casual conversation.

私は去年大学を出ました。
- I graduated from a university last year.

大学を出てないとこの国ではいい仕事を見つけるのはむずかしい。
- It's difficult to find a good job in this country unless you graduate from a university.
