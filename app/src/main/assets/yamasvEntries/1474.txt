
Transitive Verbs in Japanese

読む (to read) and 描く (to draw) are transitive verbs, so you can't say:

私は読むのが好きです。
- I like reading.

私は描くのが好きです。
- I like to draw.

You need to say 私は本を読むのが好きです/絵を描くのが好きです.

Unlike in English, you can omit the object of a transitive verb if it's obvious. You can  omit the object surrounded by parenthesis in each sentence below. You don't have to even use a pronoun.

ケンが入院しているって知ってた？
本当? 先週(彼と)会った時は元気そうだったのに。
- Did you know that Ken is in hospital.
- Really? He looked good when I saw him last week.

いつ日本に行ったの?
先月(日本に)行きました。
- When did you go to Japan?
- I went there last month.

刺し身を食べたことはありません。生の魚が美味しいとは思えない。
一度(それを)食べたら好きになりますよ。
- I have never had sashimi. I don't think that uncooked fish is good.
- You would like it if you had it once.

なぜこの授業を取ってるの？
友達から(この授業を)取るように勧められたから。
- Why are you taking this course?
- Because a friend of mine recommended that I should take it.
