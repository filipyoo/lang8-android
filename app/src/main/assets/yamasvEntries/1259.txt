
Using 〜やそこら

〜やそこら is similar to 〜かそこら I explained in the last post. It also means "or so" but やそこら is mainly used in a negative sentence.

3ヶ月やそこらで外国語はマスターできない。
- You can't master a foreign language in three months or so.

500万やそこらでこの車は売れない。
- I don't sell my car for 5 million yen or so.

このテーブルはすごく思いから、2,3人やそこらでは動かせない。
- This table is so heavy. It's not possible to move it only by two or three people.

2回やそこら失敗したくらいで諦めたらいけない。
- You shouldn't give up just because you've failed only two or so times.
