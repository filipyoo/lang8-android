
The Difference Between といい, ばいい and ほうがいい

If you want to master Japanese, you should move to Japan.
日本語をマスターしたければ、日本に住むといい。
日本語をマスターしたければ、日本に住めばいい。
日本語をマスターしたければ、日本に住んだほうがいい。

Each of the sentences above has the different nuances. 

1. 日本語をマスターしたければ、日本に住むといい。
といい comes after u-form of verbs. This is  a normal suggestion. There is no implied meaning in this sentence. 


2. 日本語をマスターしたければ、日本に住めばいい。
ばいい comes after e-form of verbs. 〜ばいい after a conditional sentence which describes your desire is used to show a necessary and sufficient condition. Therefore the sentence implies that "you would master Japanese before you knew if you lived in Japan", or "The easiest way to master Japanese is to live in Japan."
"日本に住みさえすればいい" is more explicit expression to mean this.


Here is another example using ばいい:

操作を取り消したければ、このボタンをクリックすればいい。
Your operations will be cancelled when you click this button. (You don't have to do anything else when you want to cancel your operations.)


3. 日本語をマスターしたければ、日本に住んだほうがいい。
ほうがいい comes after ta-form of verbs. たほうがいい has the nuance that "if you didn't do it, you would have a trouble." Therefore, the Japanese sentence implies that "you wouldn't master Japanese without living in Japan" or "To live in Japan is the only way to master Japanese."


Here is another example using ほうがいい:

急いだほうがいい - You should hurry. (Otherwise, you would be late.)
