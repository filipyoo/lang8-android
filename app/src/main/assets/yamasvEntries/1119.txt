
How to Say "How about...?" and "What about...?" in Japanese

When you suggest something, you can use "〜はどうですか?" in a formal conversation, or "〜はどう?" in a casual one.

日本的な場所に行ってみたいです。
京都はどうですか？古いお寺がたくさんありますよ。
- I'd like to visit a very Japanese place.
- How about Kyoto? There are a lot of traditional temples.

この漢字の読み方がわからない。わかる？
わからない。彼に聞いてみたらどう？ 日本人だし。
- I don't know how to read this kanji. Do you know?
- No. What about asking him? He's Japanese.


When you refer to a potential problem, you can say "〜はどうするのですか?" in a formal conversation, or "〜はどうするの?" in a casual one.

今夜は妻と二人だけで映画を見に行く予定。
子供達はどうするの？
両親に頼むつもり。
- My wife and I are going to go to a movie by ourselves tonight.
- What about your kids?
- I'm going to ask my parents to take care of them.

ボルシェを買いたい。
お金はどうするの？
まだ考えてない。
- I want to buy a Porsche.
- What about the money?
- I haven't thought about it.
