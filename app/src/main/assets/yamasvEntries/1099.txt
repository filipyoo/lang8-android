
How to Say "to regret to do" and "to regret doing" in Japanese

残念ながら means "to regret to."  It's followed by an action you regret to do.

残念ながら、これ以上あなたを雇えないことをお伝えしないといけません。
- We regret to inform you that we can't hire you anymore.
The action you regret to do such as 伝える(to tell) or 言う(to say) can be omitted, so you can also say "残念ながら、これ以上あなたを雇えません."

残念ながら、あなたの車は修理できません。古すぎて部品が手に入りません。
- I regret to tell you that we can't fix your car. It's too old to get the parts.


後悔している means "to regret doing something." It follows an action you regret, and you need to convert the verb into a noun by using こと. Particle を is placed after the noun.

あなたを解雇したことを後悔している。
- We regret having fired you.

去年家を売ったことを後悔している。もし売ってなければ、今なら500万円以上高い値段で売れていた。
- I regret selling my house last year. If I didn't, it would be sold for more than 5 million yen higher than then now.

その車を買ったことを後悔するだろう。
- You'll regret buying the car. 
When you talk about the future, you need to use the ta-form of the verb you regret even when you don't do the action yet. Therefore, this Japanese sentence doesn't tell you whether the listener already has bought the car or they haven't bought it yet.

あなたの愛している人とじゃなくて、金持ちだけど愛してない人と結婚したことを後悔するだろう。
- You'll regret getting married to him, who is rich, but you don't love, instead of the man you love.
