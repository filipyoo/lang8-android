
Using おかしい

おかしい means "funny."

あのコメディは本当におかしかった。
- That comedy was really funny.
You can write it as 可笑しい only in this case, but it's usually written in hiragana.


おかしい also means "wrong."

この添削はおかしい。書いた人が言いたいことと意味が違っている。
- This correction is wrong. It changes the meanings the writer really wants to say.

この数字はおかしい。これはパーセンテージだから100より小さくないといけないはず。
- This number is wrong. It should be under 100 because it shows a percentage. 


It's also used to mean "not fair."

タダで残業しないといけないのはおかしい。
- It's not fair that we have to work overtime with no pay.

お金持ちも貧乏人も同じ税率なのはおかしい。
- It's not fair that tax rates both for rich people and poor people are the same.


It's also used to mean "insane" or "ridiculous."

彼はちょっとおかしいから相手にしない方がいい。
- He's a little insane. You should ignore him.

おかしなことを言うな。
- Don't say such a ridiculous thing.
