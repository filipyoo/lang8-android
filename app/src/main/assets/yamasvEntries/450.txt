
Price-related Expressions in Japanese

When you tell prices of things, です or する follows the price. 

このチョコレートは1000円する。
このチョコレートは1000円です。
This chocolate is one thousand yen.


If you think that the price is too expensive, you can say もする.

この小さいチョコレートが3000円 もする なんて信じられない。
I can't believe that this tiny chocolate is three thousand yen.

飛行機のチケット代が3000ドルもするなら、日本に行けない。
I can't go to Japan if the plane ticket to Japan is three thousand dollars.


かかる is used for prices of costs.

この箱を東京に送るには、500円かかる。
It costs 500 yen to send this package to Tokyo.

この地域は冬はとても寒いから、暖房費がたくさんかかる。
I have to pay much heating costs because this area is so cold in winter.


かける means "to pay costs."

家をリフォームするのに100万円かけた。
I paid one million yen to renovate my house.

我々は、100億円かけて新しいマシンを開発する予定だ。
We are going to pay ten billion yen to develop new machines.


しない and かからない are used to say that the price is under some price.

この車は100万円しない。
You can buy this car under one million yen.

ここからホテルまでタクシーで1000円かからないと思う。
I think that It costs under one thousand yen to go to the hotel from here by taxi.


1万円台 technically means from 10,000 yen to 19,999 yen, and 10万円台 means from 100,000 to 199,999 yen. However, 1万円台 is mainly used to mean "close to 20,000 yen, but under 20,000 yen:" for example, 19,950 yen. If someone says that "この車は、100万円台で買える," it doesn't mean that you can buy the car by about one million yen, it actually means that you can buy it under two million yen. It may be 1,999,999 yen.
