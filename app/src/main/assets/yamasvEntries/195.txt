
The usage of まるで.

まるで has two different usages.

First, it is used to mean "as if." It is often used with ようだ or ように in this case.

この人形は、まるで生きているようだ。
This doll seems to be alive.

彼は、まるで なんでも知っているように話している。
He is talking as if he knows everything.  

彼は、まるで 別人のようになった。
He became a different man.

彼らは、まるで 双子のようだ。(This sentence implies that they are not twins.)
They look as if they are twins.


Second, it means "not at all."; it is used to deny something strongly. It is often used in a negative sentence in this case.

私には、その映画の良さが まるで わからない。
I can't understand the beauty of the picture at all.

私は、まるで 英語が話せない。
I can't speak English at all.

私は、彼とは まるで 違う。
I'm completely different to him.

彼らは双子なのに まるで 似ていない。
They don't look similar to each other at all, even though they are twins.
