
Using まず

まず means "first," which is used to explain an order.

日本語を全然知らないのなら、まずひらがなから勉強した方がいい。
- If you don't know Japanese at all, you should learn hiragana first.

商談の時は、まず軽い話題から始めたほうがいい。
- You should start with a light topic at a business negotiation.


It's also used with a negative form to make the sentence stronger.

6ヶ月で日本語をマスターすることはまずできない。
- I bet you won't be able to master Japanese for six months.

この国の人は生の魚はまず食べないから、ここでは寿司は流行らないと思う。
- People in this country never have raw fish, so I don't think that sushi will become popular here.

そのヘビに噛まれたら、まず助からない。
- If you're bitten by that snake, you'll die for sure.
