
Using 〜に〜ない

When the dictionary form of a verb is followed by に and the negative potential form of the one follows the に, it means that you want/have to do the action, but you can't. 

車がないから買い物に行くに行けない。
- I have to go shopping, but I can't because I have no car. 

大雪のせいで帰るに帰れない。
- I'd  like to go home, but I can't because of the heavy snow. 

このドレスは高すぎて買うに買えない。
- I'd like this dress, but it's too expensive for me.
