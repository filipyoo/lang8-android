
Using 何でも(なんでも)

何でも means "anything/everything." (It's usually pronounced as なんでも; なにでも sounds a little odd to me.) 

彼はすごく金持ちだから欲しいものは何でも買える。
- He's so rich that he can buy anything he wants.

彼のことは何でも知っている。
- I know everything about him.


なんでも is also used to say hearsay. It's usually placed at the begin of a sentence in this case. I think you should write it in hiragana in this case.

なんでも、彼は転勤するらしい。
- I heard that he's going to be transferred.

なんでも、彼女は三つ子を妊娠しているらしい。
- I heard that she's expecting triplets.


何が何でも means "at any costs."

何が何でも日本に行きたい。
- I'd like to go to Japan at any costs.

何が何でも医者になる。
- I'll become a doctor no matter how hard it would be.
