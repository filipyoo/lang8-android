
Usage of 〜そうだ

When そうだ comes after the past form or the plain form of verbs, it is used to describe hearsays.

昨日、マイケル・ジョーダンがこの店で昼食を食べたそうだ。
They say that Michel Jordan ate lunch at this restaurant yesterday.

彼は毎日昼食にハンバーガーを食べるそうだ。
I heard that he ate hamburgers at lunch every day.

彼女は明日社長といっしょに昼食を食べるそうだ。
I heard that she is going to eat lunch with the president tomorrow.


When そうだ comes after the masu form of verbs, it is used to describe your guesses depend on an objective reason.

彼は若いし太っているから、毎日たくさん食べそうだ。
He seems to eat a lot every day because he is young and fat.

空が暗い。雨が振り出しそうだ。
The sky is gloomy. It seems to start raining soon.


When you want to negate そうだ which is used to describe your guesses, なさそうだ is not incorrect but sounds unnatural. そうにない sounds more natural.

? 彼は甘いものは嫌いだから、このケーキは食べなさそうだ。
o 彼は甘いものは嫌いだから、このケーキは食べそうにない。
I think he won't eat this cake because he doesn't like sweets.

? 夫はすごく疲れているから、手伝ってくれなさそうだ。
o 夫はすごく疲れているから、手伝ってくれそうにない。
It seems my husband is too tired to help me.
