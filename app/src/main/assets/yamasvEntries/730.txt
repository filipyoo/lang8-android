
How to say "try to" and "try ...ing" in Japanese

When you are talking about the past event, ”the volitional form of verbs + とした" means "tried to ...," and "the te-form of verbs + 〜みた" means "tried ...ing."

ドアを開けようとしたが、鍵がかかっていた。
- I tried to open the door, but it was locked.

すごく暑かったのでドアを開けてみたが、何も変わらなかった。
- It was very hot, so I tried opening the door, but nothing was changed.

彼に頼もうとしたが、彼は家にいなかった。
- I tried to ask him, but he wasn't at home.

彼に頼んでみたが、断られた。
- I tried asking him, but he refused.


You can say "〜てみる" in both cases, when you are talking about the future event.

日本語はうまくないけど、日本語で彼に話しかけてみる。
- I'm not good at Japanese, but I'll try to talk to him in Japanese.

日本語が話せるから、日本語で彼に頼んでみる。
- I can speak Japanese, so I'll try asking him in Japanese.

この鍵があのドアの鍵かわからないけど、これで開けてみる。
- I'm not sure if this is the correct key for the door, but I'll try to open the door with it.

涼しくなるかどうかわからないけど、ドアを開けてみる。
- I'm not sure if it'll make this room cool, but I'll try opening the door.
