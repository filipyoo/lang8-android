
Using 用

用(よう) is a short form of 用事, which means a plan,something to do, or an errand. It's mainly used to refer to something not so important; if it's important, you should say 大事な用.
用がある means that you have a plan. It can be used by itself and can also follow a phrase.

今日は用があるからいっしょに遊べない。
- I've got something to do, so I can't hang out with you today. 

午後に銀行に行く用がある。
- I have a plan to go to the bank this afternoon.

You don't have to use the past tense of the verb before 用 even if you are talking about a past event. 

銀行に行く用があった。
- I had a plan to go to the bank.

用を済ます means to finish something to do.

用を済ましてから(あなたの)家に行きます。/ 用が済んだら(あなたの)家に行きます。
- I'll go to your place after finishing something to do.


用ができた means "something came up."

用ができたから帰ります。
- Something came up and I have to go. 

急用 or 急な用事 means something urgent.

ごめんなさい。急用ができたからパーティには行けません。
- I'm sorry, but I can't come to the party because something urgent came up.


用がない or 用はない means that you don't need the object.

試験に合格したからこの教科書は用がなくなった。
- I've passed the test, so I don't need this textbook any more.

今のところあなたには用はありません。
- I have nothing to ask you for now.

用はないけど電話しただけ。
- I'm just calling to say hi.
