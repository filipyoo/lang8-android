
Using はまる

はまる means to be stuck in.

渋滞にはまっていて、時間までに行けそうにない。
- I'm stuck in a traffic jam. I won't come in time.

車が深い雪にはまって全く動けなかった。
- My car was stuck in deep snow and I couldn't move at all.

I got stuck in a elevator.
In this case, はまる sounds unnatural. You should say "エレベーターに閉じ込められた."


はまる is also used to mean to be addicted to or to be into something. In this case, ハマる is often used.

マンガを読むのは子供っぽいと思ってたけど、日本に来た時にハマった。
- I thought that it was childish to read manga, but I got hooked on it when I came to Japan.

最近、日本酒にハマっていて、毎晩たくさん飲んでいる。
- I'm really into sake lately; I drink it a lot every night.

ギターにハマって、すごく高いやつを買った。
- I got addicted to guitar and bought a very expensive one.
