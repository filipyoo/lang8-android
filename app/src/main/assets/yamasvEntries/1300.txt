
Using あんまり

あんまり is a casual version of あまり. It's used to negate "much."

今日はあんまりお金を持っていない。
- I don't have much money today.

この料理はあんまりおいしくない。
- This dish isn't good.

パーティは楽しかった？
あんまり。
- Did you enjoy the party?
- Not much.


あんまり can be used as a predicate to mean "it's not fair" or "it's too much." In this case, you can't use あまり.

一回小さなミスをしただけでくびにするなんてあんまりだ。
- It's not fair to fire me just because I made a small mistake once.

週に四日も残業させるなんてあんまりだ。
- It's too much to make us work overtime four days a week
