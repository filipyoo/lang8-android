
Omitting Repeated Verbs

Unlike in English, you can't omit repeated verbs in Japanese. Therefore, it's not unnatural to say the same verb again and again.

今日買い物に行った？/ いや、行ってない。/ なんで行かなかったの?
- Did you go shopping today? / No, I didn't. / Why not?

タバコは吸いますか? / はい、吸います。
- Do you smoke? / Yes, I do.


There is an exception. When you use a suru-verb, you can say only する instead it when it appears for the second time. In this case, not omitting the repeated verb makes your sentence a little unnatural in a casual conversation.

今日、日本語の勉強した? / いや、しなかった。
- Did you study Japanese today? / No, I didn't.

今日、日本語の会話を練習しますか? / はい、します。
- Are you going to practice Japanese conversation today? / Yes, I'm.

毎日運動してますか？ / はい、してます。
- Do you exercise every day? / Yes, I do.
