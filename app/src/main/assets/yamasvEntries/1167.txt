
Using 〜たものの

ものの can follow the ta-form of verbs when what you did doesn't bring a good result.

高い日本語の教科書を買ったものの、僕には難しすぎた。
- Although I bought an expensive Japanese textbook, it was too difficult for me.

勇気を出して彼女をデートに誘ったものの、無視された。
- I found the courage to ask her out, but she ignored me.

日本人の学生に日本語で話しかけたものの、私が何を言ってるのかわからなかったようだ。
- I talked to a Japanese student in Japanese, but he didn't seem to understand what I was saying.

一日中パスポートを探したものの、見つからなかった。
- I looked for my passport whole the day, but I couldn't find it.


Speaking of 探す, you can use を not only after what you look for, but also after the place where you look for it.

何をしてるの？
iPhoneを探しているところ。リビングを探したけど見つからなかった。
台所を探したら？いつもそこに置いてるじゃない。
- What are you doing?
- I'm looking for my iPhone. I couldn't find it in the living room.
- You should do it in the kitchen. You always leave it there.


When you found something, you can say both 見つけた and 見つかった.

やっと見つけた。
やっと見つかった。
- Finally, I found it.

However, you can't say "見つけてよかった," you need to say "見つかってよかった" to mean "I was relieved to find it."
