
How To Convert An I-adjective Into An Adverb


You can convert an i-adjective into an adverb by changing い into く.

その花は美しい。
- The flower is beautiful.
その花は美しく咲いている。
- The flower is blooming beautifully. 

日本語の勉強は楽しい。
- Studying Japanese is fun.
日本語を楽しく勉強している。
- I enjoy studying Japanese.

その日本語の先生は厳しい。
- The Japanese teacher is strict.
その先生は厳しく日本語を教える。
- The teacher teaches us Japanese strictly.


Some of the く-form of i-adjectives can be followed by の.

この学校では多くの学生が日本語を勉強している。
- A lot of students study Japanese in this school.

近くのスーパーでこのお菓子を見つけた。
- I found this candy in a supermarket nearby.

私は朝早くのバスで学校に行きます。
- I'm going to school by bus early in the morning.

However, you can't say 少なくの, 美しくの, 楽しくの, etc. There is no common rule about this. You have to memorize them without thinking logically.
