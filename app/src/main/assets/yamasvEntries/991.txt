
Using 〜ことは〜けど

This is used when you acknowledge one thing, but there is another thing to consider. The same verb is placed before and after the ことは:

出張で東京に行くんだって？うらやましい。
行くことは行くけど、一泊二日だけだよ。
- I heard that you are going to Japan by business trip. I'm jealous.
- Yes, I am, but it's just two days one night.

みんなが話してるあの本もう読んだ?
読んだことは読んだけどそんなにおもしろいとは思わなかった。
- Have you read the book everybody's talking about.
- Yes, I have, but I didn't think it was so interesting.

昨夜レディガガのコンサートに行ったの?
行ったことは行ったけど、ステージからすごく離れた席だったから、彼女はよく見えなかった。
- Did you go to the Lady GaGa's concert last night?
- Yes, I did, but my seat was very far from the stage, so I couldn't see her well.


You can also use adjectives instead of verbs.

このラーメン美味しいよね？
美味しいことは美味しいけど、カロリーがすごく高そうだ。
- This ramen is delicious, isn't this?
- I agree, but it seems to have a very high calorific value. 

When you use na-adjectives, the first one ends with な and the second one ends with だ.

彼の書く字はとてもきれいだ。
きれいなことはきれいだけど、漢字がいくつかまちがっている。
- His handwriting is beautiful.
- Yes, but some kanji are incorrect.
