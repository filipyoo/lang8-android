
Using 頼む

頼む means "to ask someone to do something." ように follows the action you ask. もらう/くれる, the annoying word for Japanese learners, is often used before it. 

弟に(あなたを)車で駅まで送ってもらうように頼んでみる。
- I'll ask my brother to take you to the station with his car.

彼は仕事を手伝ってくれるように私に頼んだ。
- He asks me to help with his work.


You can also say "the object + を頼む." The action you ask can be omitted when it's implied.

昨日はすごく疲れていて料理したくなかったからピザを頼んだ。
- I was so tired and didn't want to cook yesterday, so I ordered in pizza.
(A lengthy way to say ピザを頼んだ would be ピザを配達してくれるように頼んだ.)

何か飲みますか？
コーヒーを頼みます。
- Would you like something to drink?
- I'd like coffee. Thanks.

どこを最初に掃除したらいいですか？
寝室を頼む。
- Where should I clean first?
- The bedroom.

一週間旅行するから、犬(の世話)を頼むよ。
- We are having a trip for a week. Please take care of our dog.


It can also used to mean "please." 

タクシーを呼びましょうか？
はい、頼みます。
- Would you like me to call a taxi for you?
- Yes, please.

頼むから is often used to stress "please."

殺さないでくれ。頼む！ / 頼むから殺さないでくれ！
- Don't kill me. Please!

頼むからお金貸して。
- Please lend me some money. Please.
