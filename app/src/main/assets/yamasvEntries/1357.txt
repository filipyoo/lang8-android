
Using 入る(はいる) and 入れる(いれる)

The difference between 入る and 入れる is a little confusing. 

入る(はいる) means "to enter."

あの門を通ってビルに入ってください。
- Enter the building through the gate.

ABC大学に入る予定です。
- I'm going to enter the ABC university.

You can also use it when you join a club, company or something like that as a member.

高校のテニス部に入った。
- I joined a tennis club at my high school.

ソニーに入る予定です。
- I'm going to join Sony.



入れる(いれる) means to put something in/into something.

彼があなたの財布をかばんに入れるのを見た。
- I saw he put your wallet in his bag.

車にガソリンを入れないといけない。
- I've got to put the gas in my car.
(ガス in Japanese usually means gaseous one such as natural gas or propane gas.)


Confusingly, the potential of form of 入る(はいる) is 入れる(はいれる), which means 入れる can be read as both いれる and  はいれる depends on the context. 

あの門を通ってビルに入れます(はいれます).
- You can enter the building through the gate.

鍵がないから部屋に入れない(はいれない).
- I can't get in the room because I don't have the key.

お湯が沸騰したら、塩を入れます(入れます)。
- Put salt into the water after it's boiled.

色のついたシャツと白いシャツを一緒に洗濯機に入れない(いれない)ほうがいい。色が移るよ。
- You shouldn't put color shirts and white shirts in the washing machine together. The color could stain the whites.



More confusingly, 入る(はいる) can be used like the potential form of 入れる.

- You can put five liters of water in this bottle.
If you translated this English sentence by using "you" as the subject, it would be (あなたは)この瓶に5リットルの水が入れられます. However, この瓶には5リットルの水が入ります sounds more natural. In the latter case, the subject is 水.

この箱は大きすぎて車のトランクに入らない。
- This box is too big to put in the trunk of the car.
Similarly, it sounds more natural than この箱は大きすぎて車のトランクに入れられない.
