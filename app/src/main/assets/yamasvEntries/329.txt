
The Usage of 〜といっても

といっても comes after a fact and it's followed by the unexpected another fact or situation. It can come after nouns, adjectives or verbs.

彼は、日本人といっても、アメリカで生まれて日本には行ったことがない。
Although he is a Japanese person, he was born in America and has never been to Japan.

社長といっても、従業員は５人だけです。
I'm the president of this company, but there are only five employees.

ついに日本に行けるといっても、出張で２日行くだけです。
Finally, I can go to Japan! However, it's only two days of business trip.


It can also be used in positive sentences.

試合に負けたといっても、相手は去年のチャンピオンなんだからそんなに気にすることはない.
We lost in the last game, but we don't have to mind so much because they are the champion team of the last season.

ケーブルテレビの料金が高いといっても、Jリーグの試合を全部見られることを考えると安いもんだよ。
You are complaining that the Cable TV is expensive, but I think it's a reasonable price considering that you can watch the all games in J League.

勤務時間が長いといっても、給料がいいからこの会社を辞める気はない。
Despite the fact that I have to work for a long time every day, I won't leave this company because I can earn a good salary.


といっても is an informal expression; you can use とはいえ instead of it in formal situations.
