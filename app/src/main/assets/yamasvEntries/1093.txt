
How to Say "Now you're talking" in Japanese

You can say "そう来なくっちゃ" or "待ってました" to say "Now you're talking." It's used when you agree what they suggested strongly.


何食べる？うどん？ラーメン？
もっといいものが食べたい。
神戸ビーフは？おごるよ。
そう来なくっちゃ！ / 待ってました！
- What do you want to eat? Udon? Ramen?
- I'd like a better one.
- How about Kobe beef? It's on me.
- Now you're talking!

勉強は飽きた。映画でも見に行かない？
そう来なくっちゃ！/ 待ってました！
- I'm bored studying. Why don't we go to a movie or something?
- Now you're talking!
