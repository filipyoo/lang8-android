
Using 〜なんてもんじゃない

なんてもんじゃない follows an adjective and it's used when the adjective is an understatement, but you don't know a stronger word.

今日はすごく寒いよね？
- It's very cold today, isn't it? 
寒いなんてもんじゃない。冷蔵庫の中にいるようだ。
- It goes way beyond that. I feel as if I were in a fridge.

神戸牛ってやわらかいってホント？
- Is it true that kobe beef is very soft?
やわらかいなんてもんじゃない。口の中で溶けるよ。
- That's an understatement. It melts in your mouth.

彼はサッカーが上手いなんてもんじゃない。真の天才だ。
- To say that he is good at soccer is putting it mildly. He is a real genuine player.

敬語はむずかしいなんてもんじゃない。私の知っている普通の日本語と完全に違う。
- Keigo is more than difficult. It's totally different from ordinary Japanese language I know.
