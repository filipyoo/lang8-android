
Using ように After a Verb

When ように follows the potential form of verbs, it means "so that."

日本語がうまく話せるように一生懸命勉強します。
I'll study Japanese hard so that I can speak it fluently.

小さい子供でも読めるように、ひらがなで書いた。
I wrote it in hiragana so that even little children could read it.

どこでも仕事ができるようにラップトップを買った。
I bought an laptop so that I could work anywhere.



ように is also used to mean "like someone does." It follows the dictionary form of verbs in this case.

日本人が話すように流暢に日本語を話したい。
I'd like to speak Japanese fluently like native speakers do.

イルカが泳ぐように泳ぎたい。
I'd like to swim like dolphins do.

私がしたようにしてみなさい。
Just do it like I did.



ように is also used to ask or instruct something. It follows the dictionary form of verbs in this case too.

11時までには寝るように。
Go to bed by eleven o'clock at the latest.

上司から、この仕事を金曜日までに終わらせるように言われている。
Our boss said that I have to finish this job by Friday.

医者からもっとたくさん野菜を摂るように言われた。
The doctor told me that I should eat more vegetables.
