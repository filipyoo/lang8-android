
Using 気になる

気になる has some different meanings:

1. To be curious about.

アップルウオッチが気になる。早く実物を見てみたい。
- I'm curious about Apple watch. I'd like to see the real thing soon.

来月オープンする日本食レストランが気になる。ラーメンを出してくれればいいのに。
- I'm curious about a Japanese food restaurant, which is going to newly open next month. I hope they'll offer ramen. 

日本人は私の国をどう思っているのか気になる。
- I'm curious about what Japanese people think of my country.


2. To be worried/concerned about.

どうしても入りたい会社の面接を受けた。結果が気になる。
- I had a job interview with a company I really want to work for. I'm worried about the result.

決勝戦は明日だ。天気が気になる。
- The final match is to be held tomorrow. I'm concerned about the weather.


3. To wonder.

彼が来るか気になる。
- I wonder if he will come.

このミステリー小説を読むのをやめられない。誰が弁護士を殺したのか気になる。知ってても言うなよ。
- I can't stop reading this mystery story. I wonder who killed the lawyer. Don't tell me who did it even if you know.


4. To be interested in someone.

It's mainly used when you are interested in the opposite sex.

転校してきた女の子が気になっている。
- I'm interested in a girl who was transferred from another school.
(The speaker is a boy.)

気がある is a stronger expression than 気になる. It's used when you want to date with someone.

彼女は君に気があるよ。デートに誘ったら?
- I think she is interested in you. Why don't you ask her out?


5. Something bothers me.

時計の音が気になって眠れない。
-  The ticking of the clock bothers me. I can't sleep!

テレビの音が気になって勉強できない。ボリューム下げてくれる?
- The sound of the TV disturbs my study. Can you turn the volume down?

前髪が気になる。今日美容院に行こう。
- My bangs are bothering me. I'll go to a hair salon today.
