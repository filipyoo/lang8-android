
Usage of まし

"AはBよりまし" means "A is better than B." However, you can't use it when you praise something. まし is used when you compare two bad things.
この店の料理は、妻の料理よりましだ。
This Japanese sentence means that the dishes in this restaurant are terrible, but they are better than his wife's cooking.

彼の髪型は、前よりましだ。
- His new hair style looks bad, but it's a little better than previous one.

君のこの前のテストの成績は、彼よりましだ。
- You got a bad score on the last test, but your score is higher than his one.
