
Very Simple Way to Use "いる/ある" Properly.

The Japanese learner sometimes makes a mistake when they use "いる/ある."

NG : 庭に犬がある　庭にブランコがいる　木に鳥がある　公園がいる

There is a simple way to use them properly. If it can move of its own will. You can say "いる."  
犬がいる　人がいる　鳥がいる

If it can't move of its own will. You should say "ある."
ブランコがある 公園がある

I know interesting examples. We say "タクシーがいる.", because the taxi driver on it and he can move taxi. If the taxi is parked without a driver, we say "タクシーがある."

If you find a man who collapses on the street, you should call the police and say "倒れている人が 'います' !".  If the man is already dead, you should say "死体が  'あります'  !"
