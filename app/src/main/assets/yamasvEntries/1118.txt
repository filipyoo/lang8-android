
Using どれくらいですか？

You can say "どれくらいですか?" to ask about length, weight, or something like this. You can simply say "どれくらい?" in a casual conversation.

身長はどれくらいですか？
- How tall are you?

駅までどれくらいですか？
- How much farther to the station?


You can also use it to ask someone about how long time has passed since something happened. In this case, it follows the te-form of verbs. You can answer the question with ”になります."

結婚してどれくらいですか？
10年になります。
- How long have you been married?
- For 10 years.

日本にきてどれくらいですか？
5年になります。
- How long have you been in Japan?
- For 5 years.


When you ask about a cost or time, you should say "どれくらいかかりますか?”

この車を修理するのに時間はどれくらいかかりますか？
- How long will it take to fix the car?

この車を修理するのに値段はどれくらいかかりますか？
- How much will it take to fix the car?

If it's obvious that whether what you are asking about is the cost or the time from the context, you can simply say "この車を修理するのにどれくらいかかりますか？"
