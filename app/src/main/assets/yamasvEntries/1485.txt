
Using 〜とか

とか is used to list examples:

子供の頃はいつもアニメを見ていた。ポケモンとかどらえもんとかドラゴンボールとか。
- When I was a kid, I was always watching anime, such as Pokemon, Doraemon or Dragon Ball.

It can also follow a phrase.

日本に行ったらやりたいことがたくさんある。本物のラーメンを食べるとか、渋谷の交差点に行くとか。
- I have a lot of things I want to do when I visit Japan, such as eating real ramen or visiting Shibuya crossing.


It can also be used when you are talking about a rumor about the listener.

来月結婚するとか。
- I heard that you are getting married next month.

息子さんが司法試験に合格なさったとか。おめでとうございます。
- I heard your son passed the National Bar Examination. Congratulations.

You can use both a rising intonation or a flat intonation. When you use a rising intonation, it sounds as if you are not sure whether the rumor is true or not.
