
The Usage of ばかり

When ばかり follows the te-form of verbs, it means "always / often 〜."

彼はコーヒーを飲んでばかりいる。
- He always drinks coffee.

彼は日本に行ってばかりいる。
- He often goes to Japan.


When ばかり follows the ta-form of verbs, it means that you've just finished the action.

コーヒーを飲んだばかりです。
- I've just finished drinking a cup of coffee.

日本に行ったばかりです。
- Recently, I went to Japan. / I've just returned from Japan.
(You can use this Japanese expression even if you returned from Japan one or two months ago.)

結婚したばかりです
- We're newlyweds.


When ばかり follows a noun, it means "only."

彼はコーヒーばかり飲んでいる。
- He drinks only coffee. / He doesn't drink anything except coffee.

この店のお客は女性ばかりです。
- Almost all of the customers of this shop are women.


ばかり can be used to mean "about."

その病院へは10分ばかりかかります。
- It takes about 10 minutes to the hospital.

私は日本に３年ばかり住んでました。
- I've lived in Japan before for about three years.
