
Omitting a Repeated Verb

When the same verbs are used in one sentence, you can omit the first one.

私は東京に3年、大阪に5年住んでました。
I lived in Tokyo for three years and Osaka for five years.


This rule applies when the subjects of the verbs are different.

彼はコーヒーを、彼女は紅茶を注文しました。
- He ordered coffee and she ordered tea.

鯨は海に、鯉は池に住んでいます。
- Whales live in the sea and carp live in ponds.

彼は東京で英語を、彼女は大阪でフランス語を教えています。
- He teaches English in Tokyo and she teaches French in Osaka.

This way is mainly used in written Japanese. In a conversation, I wouldn't omit the first verb.



When you answer a question and the verb is clear from the context, you can omit the verb.

兄弟はどこに住んでいるの？
兄はニューヨークで、弟はロンドンです。
- Where do your brothers live?
- My older brother lives in NewYork and younger one lives in London.
