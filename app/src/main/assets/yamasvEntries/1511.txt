
Using ～と称する

～と称(しょう)する means "to purport." It's often used that the speaker doesn't think what they say is true. It can follow both a noun or phrase.

刑事と称する男がやってきて、君のことを訪ねていた。
- A man purported to be a detective came and asked about you.
It implies that the speaker doesn't think that the man is a real detective.

宝くじに当たった後、遠い親戚だと称する人たちからたくさん電話がかかってきた。
- After I had won the lottery, I received a lot of call from people who purported to be my remote relatives.

幽霊と話すことができると称する男がテレビに出ている。
- A man who claims that he can talk to a ghost is on TV.

その自動車メーカーはこの車は350馬力あると称している。
- The car maker claims that this car has 350 horsepower(, but I don't believe it).
