
Using 〜てでも

The te-form of verbs + でも is used when you want/have to do something by all means. It follows an absurd action.

徹夜してでも、明日までにこの仕事を終わらせないといけない。
- I must finish this job by tomorrow, even if I have to work all night.

家を売ってでも、あのポルシェが欲しい。
- I'd like that Porsche, even if I have to sell my house.

５万円払ってでも、その料理は食べる価値がある。
- The dish is worth to pay 50,000 yen.
(This Japanese sentence doesn't tell you whether the price of the dish is actually 50,000 yen or not.)

弁護士の仕事を辞めてでも、ここで君と暮らしたい。
- I want to live here with you, even if I have to give up my carrier as a lawyer.

お金を払ってでも、ずっと尊敬している彼の下で働きたい。
- I'd like to work for him, I have always respected, even if I have to pay money.
