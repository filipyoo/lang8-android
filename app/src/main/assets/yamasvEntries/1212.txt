
Using 〜知らず

知らず can follow a noun to mean you haven't experienced something or you are insensitive to something.

子供の頃はケンカでは負け知らずだった。
- When I was a child, I haven't lost the fight.

トマトを毎日食べれば、病気知らずの身体になれる。
- If you eat tomatoes every day, you can keep good health.

私は自転車で会社に通っているので、渋滞知らずだ。
- Since I go to my office by bike, I don't get into a traffic jam.

彼は怖いもの知らずだ。勇敢なのかそれとも馬鹿なのかはわからない。
- He doesn't fear anything. I don't know it's because he's brave or because he's stupid.

お前はなんて恥知らずなんだ。
- How shameless you are.

彼女は大金持ちの娘で世間知らずだ。電車の切符の買い方も知らない。
- She's a daughter of a billionaire and ignorant of the world; she doesn't even know how to get a train ticket.
