
How to Say "have/let/make someone do something" in Japanese

てもらう is the best way to say "have someone do something."

彼女に髪を切ってもらうつもりです。
- I'm going to have her cut my hair.

シェフに一番人気がある料理を作ってもらった。
- I had the chef cook a most popular dish in the restaurant.

せる is technically correct to mean "have someone do something."
彼女に髪を切らせるつもりです。
シェフに一番人気がある料理を作らせた。
However, it sounds bossy. You shouldn't say like this.

You can say せる if you were a boss of them and you have them do something for someone else.
彼女にあなたの髪を切らせるつもりです。
- I'm going to have her cut your hair.
シェフに一番人気がある料理を作らせた。
- I had the chef cook a  most popular dish in the restaurant for a guest.



せる sounds natural to say "let someone do something."

お願いだからパーティに行かせて。
- Let me go to the party, please.

私はいつも彼に車を使わせている。
- I let him use my car whenever he wants.



せる is also used to mean "make someone do something." 

彼に行かせます。
- I'll make him go.

昨日は彼に残業させた。
- I made him work overtime yesterday.

You can also say てもらう even if they don't want to do it and can't ignore your order.
彼に行ってもらいます。
昨日は彼に残業してもらった。
