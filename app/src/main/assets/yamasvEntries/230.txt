
The Usage of 気です

〜気です has almost the same meaning as "〜つもりです."

My father is going to buy a new car.
父は、新しい車を買う気です.
= 父は、新しい車を買うつもりです.

He is going to give her an expensive ring.
彼は、彼女に高い指輪をあげる気です。
= 彼は、彼女に高い指輪をあげるつもりです。


However, unlike つもりです, you can't use 気です to describe your intention.
o 私は、新しい車を買うつもりです.
x 私は、新しい車を買う気です.


As with つもりです, you should avoid using 気です to ask the other person's intention. "いつ来る気ですか?" and "いつ来るつもりですか?" sometimes sound as if you are blaming the other person. You should say "いつ来ますか?" if you merely want to know the other person's schedule.

"How long will you sleep?" is a literal translation of "いつまで寝ている気? / いつまで寝ているつもり?" However these Japanese sentences are used to mean "Get out of bed right now!"
