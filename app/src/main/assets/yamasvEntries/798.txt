
Using 〜っこない

〜っこない is a casual version of "〜るはずがない," which means that it's impossible to do. You need to replace る in the potential form of verbs with っこない.

こんな複雑な数学の公式覚えられっこない。
You can't memorize this complicated math formula.

こんな苦いコーヒー飲めっこない。
This coffee is too bitter; I can't drink it.

7時までには着けっこない。
It's impossible to arrive there by seven o'clock.

そんな高い車買えっこない。
I can't buy such an expensive car.


You may have noticed that the particles after the objects are omitted in some of the sentences above. They are often omitted in a casual conversation.
