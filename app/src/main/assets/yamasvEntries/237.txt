
The Usage of 〜せいか and 〜のか.

〜せいか means "probably because." It comes after verbs.

香川が出場しない せいか、今日の観客はいつもより少ない.
There're less spectators than usual, probably because Kagawa won't play today.

広告の予算を減らした せいか、その車はあまり売れていない。
The car isn't sold too much, probably because they cut advertising budgets.


It can also come after nouns. You have to add の after the noun in this case.

雨 のせいか、客が全く来ない
Customers don't come at all, probably because of the rain.

怪我 のせいか、彼は活躍しなかった。
He didn't make a good performance, probably because of his injury.

You can use ためか instead of せいか.
香川が出場しない ためか、今日の観客はいつもより少ない.
広告の予算を減らした ためか、その車はあまり売れていない。
雨の ためか、客が全く来ない
怪我の ためか、彼は活躍しなかった。


The usage of のか is similar with せいか. However のか always comes after not facts but your guesses .

全部の試験が終った のか、彼女はうれしそうだ。
She looks happy. I guess she's finished all exams.

事故でもあった のか、大勢の人が集まっている。
There's a crowd over there. I guess some accident happens.


In order to make clear the difference between せいか and のか, I'll compare two sentences. 

仕事の面接がうまく行かなかった せいか、彼女はいつもより口数が少ない。
仕事の面接がうまく行かなかった のか、彼女はいつもより口数が少ない。

In the first sentence, the speaker knows that she's failed the job interview, and he's guessing that that's why she doesn't speak too much than usual. 
In the second sentence, the speaker doesn't know the result of her job interview, but he's guessing that she's failed the job interview by the look of her.
