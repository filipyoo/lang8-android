
Using 〜かねる

When かねる follows the masu stem of a verb, it means "cannot." It's used not when you don't have the ability, but when you cannot do something for some reasons or when you describe your opinion.

彼の言うことには賛成しかねる。
- I can't agree with him. 

お客さんの秘密を守る義務があるので、その質問にはお答えしかねます。
- I can't answer the question because I have an obligation to keep my customers' secret.

彼女は頭がいいとは言いかねる。
- I can't say that she is smart. / I don't think she is smart.

父親にどの贈り物を買うか決めかねている。
- I hesitate which gift to buy for my father.


On the other hand, 〜かねない is used when something might happen. It's mainly used in negative sentences.

彼女は、私達の秘密を話しかねない。
- She would reveal our secret.

彼は、冷蔵庫のビールを一人で全部飲みかねない。
- He would drink whole beers in the refrigerator by himself.

その医者は、患者を死なせかねない重大なミスをした。
- The doctor made a serious mistake that could kill a patient.
