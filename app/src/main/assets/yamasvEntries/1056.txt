
How Do You say "What happens if...." casually in Japanese

"もし～たら何が起きる" is a literal translation of "what happens if...," but it sounds slightly exaggerated in a casual conversation. "～たらどうなる" sounds more natural.

断ったらどうなる？
- What happens if I say no?

電子レンジで卵を温めたらどうなる？
- What happens if you microwave eggs?

コーラの瓶にミントキャンディーを入れたらどうなるか知ってる？
- Do you know what happens if you drop a mint candy into a bottle of coke?


どうなった is used to ask about a result casually.

それはどうなった？
- How did it turn out?


どうなってる is used to ask about a situation casually.

そのプロジェクトはどうなってる？
- How's the project going?


You can say どうする to ask "what if you....?"

宝くじに当たったらどうする？
- What if you win the lottery?

私が断ってたらどうしてたの？
- What would you have done if I said no?



何があった is a natural way to say "What happened?"

何があったの？ ("どうしたの?" also sounds fine.)
- What happened to you?



あった can follow a noun that indicates a specific event. It sounds more natural than 起きた in a casual conversation. Particle が is used before あった.

昨日また大きな地震があった。
- A big earthquake happened again yesterday.

家(うち)の近くで火事があった。
- A fire happened near my house.

会社で殺人事件があった。
- A murder happened in my office.
