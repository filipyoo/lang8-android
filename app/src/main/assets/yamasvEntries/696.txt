
Using 〜というのに

〜というのに means "even though 〜." It's followed by an unexpected situation. This いう has nothing to do with "to say." When it follows a noun, だ is placed before it.

もう秋だというのに暑すぎる。
- It's too hot, even though it's already fall.

明日試験があるというのに、弟はずっとゲームをしている。
- My brother has been playing video games, even though he'll have a test tomorrow.

この試合をずっと楽しみにしてたというのに、雨で中止になった。
- Even though I have been looking forward to this game for a long time, it's cancelled because of the rain.

雨だというのに子どもたちが公園でサッカーをしている。
- Children are playing soccer in the park in spite of the rain.
