
Using 甲斐

甲斐 can follow the masu stem or ta-form of some verbs to mean that "it's worth to 〜." When it follows the masu stem of a verb, it's pronounced as がい. When it follows ta-form of a verb, it's pronounced as かい.



自動運転技術は研究し甲斐(がい)がある。
- Self-driving technology is worth researching.

彼らは頭がいいし、一生懸命勉強するから教え甲斐(がい)がある。
- It's fun to teach them because they are smart and study hard.

今の仕事はやり甲斐(がい)がない。
- My current job isn't very rewarding.

富士山はすごく綺麗だった。行った甲斐(かい)があった。
- Mt. Fuji was so beautiful. It was worth going there.

試験に受かった。勉強した甲斐(かい)があった。
- I've passed the exam. My studies have paid off.


甲斐 after the dictionary form of a verb sounds unnatural. 価値 sounds natural when it follows the dictionary form of a verb.

その映画は見る価値がある。
- The movie is worth watching.

彼らのサービスにはお金を払う価値がない。
- It's not worth paying money for their service.



応え(ごたえ) is also able to follow the masu stem of some verbs and can be used when you are satisfied with doing it.

決勝戦はすごく面白くて見応えがあった。
- The final match was great. It was worth watching.

この本はほんとに読み応えがある。
- This book is really worth reading.

彼の演説は聴き応えがあった。
- His speech was worth listening to.


食べ応え is mainly used when you are satisfied with the amount rather than the taste.
