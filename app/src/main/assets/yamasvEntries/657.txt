
Using 否応(いやおう)なく

否応なく or 否応なしに is used when you have no choice. (The kanji 否 means "no," and 応 means "yes." ) It's a formal expression and mainly used in negative sentences.

この国では、政府に反対する人は皆 否応なしに/否応なく 逮捕される。
- Every person who expresses negative opinions against the government  is arrested forcibly in this country.

彼らは我々を一日に14時間 否応なく/否応なしに 働かせた。
- They made us work 14 hours a day forcibly.


否が応でも modifies the whole sentence which follows it.

否が応でも、金曜日までに借金を払わないといけない。
- You must pay your deposit by Friday whether you want to or not.

否が応でも、この商品を売らないといけない。
- You must sell all these goods out whether you want to or not.

否が応でも、人はいずれ死ぬ。
- Every person dies someday whether they like it or not.
