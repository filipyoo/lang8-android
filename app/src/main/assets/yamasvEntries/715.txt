
How to say "as long/far as" In Japanese

You can say 〜限り to mean "as long/far as." It follows the dictionary form of verbs.

ここにいる限り安全です。
- You are safe as long as you stay here.

私の知る限り、この街には日本人は住んでいない。
- As far as I know, no Japanese people live in this town.

タバコを 吸う限り、その病気は治らないでしょう。
- You couldn't recover from the disease as long as you smoke.

タバコを吸わない限り、ここに居ていい。
- You can stay here as long as you don't smoke.
(This Japanese sentence is the same as "タバコさえ吸わなければ、ここに居ていい.")


"As 〜 as you want/can" is translated to "〜だけ."

居たいだけここに居ていい。
- You can stay here as long as you want.

冷蔵庫のビールを飲みたいだけ飲んでいい。
- You can drink beer in the refrigerator as much as you want.

できるだけ長くここに居たい。
- I want to stay here as long as I can.


You can also say 好きなだけ instead of 〜たいだけ.
好きなだけここに居ていい。
冷蔵庫のビールを好きなだけ飲んでいい。
