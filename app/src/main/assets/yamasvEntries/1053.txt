
Using だけ and しか〜ない

"All he wants is money."
Google translator may translate this sentence into "彼が欲しい全てはお金だ," but it sounds strange. "彼が欲しいのはお金だけ" sounds natural. You should use だけ to translate  "All S + V is O" into Japanese in many cases.

この部屋から見えるのは砂漠だけだ。
- All I can see is desert from this room.

この一ヶ月で読んだのはマンガだけだ。
- All I've read in the past one month is manga.

両親に会ってくれるだけでいい。
- All you have to do is meet my parents.

私の話を聞いて欲しいだけ。
- All I want you to do is listen to me.




Conversely, "しか〜ない" sounds natural to translate "only" when the predicate is a verb, while the dictionary says that "'only' means だけ."

私の部屋にはベッドしかない。
- There is only bed in my room.

1000円しか持ってない。
- I have only 1,000 yen.

私は英語しか話せない。
- I speak only English.
("私は英語しか話さない" sounds like "Although I can speak other languages, I don't want to speak any language but English.")  

You should use だけ when the predicate is a noun. In this case, it sounds as if you want to focus the noun as a topic.
私の部屋にあるのはベッドだけだ。
私が持っているのは1000円だけだ。
私が話せるのは英語だけだ。
