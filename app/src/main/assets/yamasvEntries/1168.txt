
Using たかが

たかが is followed by something you don't think it's valuable or important.

たかが一回の失敗で落ち込むことはない。
- You shouldn't be dissapointed by just one failure.

たかが洋服に高いお金を払う人が理解できない。
- I can't understand people who pay a lot of money for just clothes.

ボスはたかが3分会議に遅れただけで怒った。
- My boss got angry just because I was late for the meeting only for 3 minutes.

ほんとうにごめん。君の車をぶつけてしまった。
たかが車だ。どうってことはない。それより君が怪我しなくてよかった。
- I'm so sorry. I crashed your car.
- It's just a car. That's not important. I was relieved you weren't  injured.
