
一方 (いっぽう)

It is used as a conjunction, and also used as a noun. You can add で after it when you use it as a conjunction.


1.1
When it is used as a conjunction, it means “whereas” or “on the other hand.” It is used to compare two completely different things. 

私はゲームに興味はないが、一方で、弟はゲームが大好きだ。
I am not interested in  video games, whereas my little brother loves them.

アリが一生懸命働いている 一方で、キリギリスは遊んでいた。
While the ants were working hard,  the grasshopper was playing.

彼は金持ちになった一方で、親友を失った。
While he became rich, he lost his best friend.


1.2
You can use it to list two things occurring in parallel.

私は大学に通う 一方で、エンジニアとしても働いている。
I am attending a university while working as an engineer.

その店は、昼はカフェとして営業する 一方、夜はバーとして営業している。
That is open as a cafe during the day and a bar on evenings. 

彼は才能のあるサッカー選手である 一方、いい父親でもある。
He is a gifted football player, and he also is a good father.


2.
一方 is used as a noun. 一方だ is used to describe that the changes will continue to process in one direction. It follows a verb which describes the change.

妻が浪費家なので、借金が増える 一方だ。
Since my wife is a big spender, our debts go on increasing.

彼女の日本語は上達する 一方だ。
Her Japanese ability goes on improving.

海外の人と連絡をとるのは簡単になる 一方だ。
It will become more and more easy to communicate with people who lives abroad.
