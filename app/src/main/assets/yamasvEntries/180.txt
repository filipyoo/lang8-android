
The difference between はずではない and はずがない.

I would like to explain the difference between はずでない and はずがない.

(The difference between はずがない and はずでない is very slight, so I won't explain that.)

はずではない and はずがない aren't interchangeable.

はずではない means "not expected to / not have a plan to."

彼はそのパーティでスピーチする はずではない。
- He isn't expected to make a speech at that party.

彼はそのパーティでスピーチする はずではなかった。
- He wasn't expected to make a speech at that party.
(This sentence in Japanese implies that he made a speech.)

彼は日本で働く はずではなかった。
- He didn't have a plan to work in Japan.
(This sentence in Japanese implies that he worked in Japan before or he is working in Japan now.)

はずじゃない is a casual expression of はずではない. 
こんなはずじゃなかった is a popular phrase when something ends with unexpected results.


はずがない is used to describe impossibilities. It can follow the past tense and the present tense.

彼が誰かの手助けなしにそれをできた はずがない。
He can't have done it without someone's help.

彼は入院中なので、昨日そのレストランにいた はずがない。
It was impossible that he was in the restaurant yesterday because he was in a hospital.

その問題はほとんどの大学生にも解けないので、小学生に解ける はずがない。
It's hardly possible that elementary school children solve the problem because most university students can't.

In the above three sentences, you can use はずはない instead of はずがない.
