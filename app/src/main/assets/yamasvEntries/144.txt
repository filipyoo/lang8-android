
The usage of ては.

ては is used to express "must" or "have to." It must be followed by negative form.

それを食べてはいけない。(食べちゃだめ/食べちゃいけない are casual expressions.)
You must not eat that. 
 
それを食べなくてはいけない。(食べなくちゃだめ/食べなくちゃいけない are casual expressions.)
You must eat that. / You have to eat that. 
 
そこに行ってはいけない。(行っちゃだめ/行っちゃいけない are casual expressions.)
You must not go there. 
 
そこに行かなくてはいけない。(行かなくちゃだめ/行かなくちゃいけない are casual expressions.)
You must go there. / You have to go there.


You can end your sentence with ては when you have/need to do something.

もっと勉強しなくては。(もっと勉強しなくちゃ/もっと勉強しないと are casual expressions.)
I have to study hard.

明日は早く起きなくては。(明日は早く起きなくちゃ/明日は早く起きないと are casual expressions.)
I have to get up early tomorrow morning.

It sounds a little unnatural to end a sentence with ては when you give an advice to other people.
△ あなたは、もっと勉強しなくては。
△ あなたは、もっと早く起きなくては。



You can also use ては as a conditional conjunction; it is mainly followed by negative expression.

たくさん食べてばかりいては、太ってしまう。
You will get extra weight, if you always eat too much.

ゆっくりしていては、遅れてしまう。
I will be late if I don't hurry up.

疲れていては、いい仕事はできない。
You can't achieve good results if you are tired.
