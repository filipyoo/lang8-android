
Using ～ところだった

When ところだった follows the dictionary form of verbs, it means "was/were about to do something" or "almost did something."

彼が電話してきた時は寝るところだった。
- I was about to go to bed when he called me.

運転しながら寝るところだった。
- I almost slept while driving.

If you want to clarify that you almost did something negative, you can add あやうく or もう少しで.

あやうく/もう少しで シャツにコーヒーをこぼすところだった。
- I almost spilled my coffee on my shirt.

あやうく/もう少しで 事故で死ぬところだった。
- I almost died in the accident.
If you just said that "死ぬところだった," it could sound as if you were about to commit suicide.

If you almost did something positive, you can't use あやうく.

もう少しでテストに受かるところだった。
- I almost possed the test.
If you say あやうくテストに受かるところだった, it implies that you didn't want to pass it.



When ところだった follows the past form of verbs, it means that you just finished do the action.

彼が昼食に誘った時は、ちょうど昼食を食べたところだった。
- I just finished lunch when he asked me to go out for lunch.
