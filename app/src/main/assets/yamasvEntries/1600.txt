
Using ほど

ほど is used to mean "the more ～, the more ～" or something like this.

たくさん話すほど、日本語は上達する。
- The more you speak, the more your Japanese will improve.

高く登るほど、寒くなる。
- The higher you climb, the cooler it will be.


ほど is also used to compare with something. It's usually used with ない, a negative form.

私の日本語は彼ほど上手くない。
- My Japanese is not so good as him.

運動の後のビールほどいいものはない。
- Nothing is better than beer after workout.

When you say "as ～" with a positive form, you should say くらい.

彼くらい日本語が上手ければいいのに。
- I wish my Japanese was so good as him.
彼ほど日本語が上手ければいいのに sounds a little unnatural.


ほど can follow a phrase to mean "so much that."

私は週に4,5回ここに来るほど、このレストランが好きだ。
- I love this restaurant so much that I come here four or five times a week.

野球は好きだけど、スタジアムに行くほどではない。
- I like baseball, but not so much that I go to the stadium. 



ほど can also follow a number to mean "about." It sounds a little more formal than くらい.

10分ほどで着きます。
- It'll take about 10 minutes to get there.

日本までのチケットの値段は2000ドルほどです。
- The price of the ticket to Japan is about 2,000 dollars.

日本に10回ほど行ったことがあります。
- I've visited Japan about ten times.
