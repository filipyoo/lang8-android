
Expressions Using 話(はなし)

話があります。
- We need to talk. / I have something to discuss with you.



話にならない means "out of the question / far from acceptable."

月3万円でその家を借りられますか。
それでは話にならない。少なくとも月5万円はいただかないと。
- Can I rent the house for 30,000 yen per month?
- It's out of the question. You need to pay at least 50,000 yen a month.



話をつける means "to negotiate."

彼と値段について話をつけてくる。
- I'll negotiate the fee with him."



話がつく means "to reach a conclusion" or "to agree" after a negotiation.

月4万円で話がついた。
- We agreed that I pay 40,000 yen per month.



話が合う is used when two people have so much in common.

彼とは話が合うから、仕事帰りによく飲みに行く。
- He and I have the same taste. We often go drinking with him after work. 

彼はいい人だけど、話が合わない。
- Although he's a nice person, I don't get along with him in talk.



You can say 話が見える/見えない to mean "can/can't understand what they are talking about."

話が見えない。
- What are you talking about? / I don't understand what you are talking about.

やっと話が見えてきた。
- I finally understand what they are talking about.



話がなくなる means that what you have decided is called off.

東京に転勤になるんだって？
残念だけどその話はなくなった。
- I heard that you are going to be transferred to Tokyo.
- Unfortunately, it was called off.



おいしい話 means "juicy story" / "lucrative deal."

日曜日に可愛い女の子に英語を教えている。カフェで一時間その子とおしゃべりするだけで1万円もらえる。
それはおいしい話だ。
- I teach a pretty girl English on Sundays. I can earn 10,000 yen by just chatting with her in a cafe for one hour.
- That's a juicy deal.
