
Using 〜倍

When 倍(ばい) follows a number, it means "times."

日本の人口は私の国の3倍です。
- Japan's population is three times our country's.

彼の車の値段は私の車の5倍する。
- His car is five times as expensive as mine.
(When you are talking about a price, you can use する after 倍.)

バスで行くとタクシーより3倍時間がかかる。
- It takes three times as long if you go bus as if you go by taxi.


The opposite of it is 〜分(ぶん)の1.

私の国の人口は日本の3分の1です。
- Our country's population is one-third times Japan's.

私の車の値段は彼の車の5分の1です。
- My car is one-fifth times as expensive as his.

タクシーで行くとバスの3分の1の時間で行ける。
- It takes one-third times as long if you go by taxi as if you go by bus.
(In this case, かかる sounds unnatural because かかる implies that it takes more time.)
